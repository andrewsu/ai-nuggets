# Scripps Biomedical Brief — 2026-06-07

## Script

Good morning. Today's brief: first-in-class small-molecule inhibitors of herpesvirus alkaline nucleases with broad activity across alpha, beta, and gamma human herpesviruses from Sandra Weller's group at the University of Connecticut, collecting-duct-targeted lipid nanoparticles delivering P-K-D-two messenger R-N-A that reverse established cystic disease in autosomal dominant polycystic kidney disease from Eun Ji Chung's group at U-S-C, an autonomous closed-loop microfluidic platform driven by agentic A-I that optimized enzyme cascades for seven days without human intervention from Zbynek Prokop's group at Masaryk University with Stavros Stavrakis and Andrew deMello at E-T-H Zurich, and desert hedgehog identified as an endothelial resilience factor that restrains endothelial-mesenchymal transition and protects against atherosclerosis from Suowen Xu's group at the University of Science and Technology of China.

First, from Neha Sharma, Xiaoyu Xie and colleagues with Sandra Weller at the University of Connecticut, the first viral nuclease inhibitors targeting a conserved class of essential herpesvirus replication enzymes. The H-S-V-one U-L-twelve gene encodes a five-prime-to-three-prime alkaline exonuclease that collaborates with the single-stranded D-N-A binding protein I-C-P-eight to drive recombination-dependent viral replication, and orthologs are essential in every other human herpesvirus — U-L-ninety-eight in H-C-M-V and S-O-X in K-S-H-V are conserved across the alpha, beta, and gamma subfamilies. The team solved the first crystal structure of an alphaherpesvirus alkaline nuclease — U-L-twelve point five — and used it to drive structure-activity relationships, producing inhibitors with nanomolar to low-micromolar E-C-fifties and demonstrated antiviral activity against H-S-V-one and H-C-M-V in cell culture. The same chemistry inhibits the H-C-M-V and K-S-H-V orthologs, supporting a pan-herpesvirus small-molecule strategy either as standalone therapy or in combination with nucleoside analogs against viruses where resistance to existing agents is a growing clinical problem.

Second, from Joshua Giblin, Karen Lambaren and colleagues with Eun Ji Chung at the University of Southern California, a collecting-duct-targeted lipid nanoparticle that delivers P-K-D-two messenger R-N-A directly to the renal collecting-duct epithelia where cysts originate in autosomal dominant polycystic kidney disease. A-D-P-K-D is caused by mutations in P-K-D-one or P-K-D-two that leave cells deficient in polycystin-one or polycystin-two protein, and no approved therapy restores polycystin expression; tolvaptan, the only approved intervention, targets downstream signaling rather than the genetic defect. The team incorporated a collecting-duct-targeting peptide into the lipid nanoparticle, drove renal accumulation over non-targeted formulations, and in a P-K-D-two-deficient mouse model repeated administration reversed established cystic disease, restored renal architecture, and reduced fibrotic and inflammatory markers without detectable off-target toxicity. A single dose also reduced cyst burden and improved renal function in the P-K-D-one-deficient background. This is the first organ-targeted messenger R-N-A gene replacement to reverse rather than slow A-D-P-K-D in vivo across both genetic backgrounds — a credible alternative to A-A-V-based gene therapy for a disease where dosing-to-organ has been the rate-limiter.

Third, from Michal Vasina, David Kovar and colleagues with Zbynek Prokop at Masaryk University, working with Stavros Stavrakis and Andrew deMello at E-T-H Zurich and Stanislav Mazurenko, CascadeMAP — an autonomous closed-loop microfluidic platform that combines high-throughput droplet microfluidics with Bayesian optimization and a multi-agent A-I system to optimize enzyme cascades end to end. They validated it on two cascades — a fluorescence-monitored glycerol detection pathway and a label-free Raman-spectroscopy-monitored one-two-three trichloropropane degradation pathway — two orthogonal detection modalities. Bayesian optimization identified optimal conditions three times faster than classical Design of Experiments. The multi-agent A-I system automated hypothesis generation across eleven gigabytes of experimental data, ran pattern recognition, and synthesized insights. Running for seven days without human intervention, CascadeMAP processed approximately two hundred twenty thousand reactions across seventy-four hundred different conditions — a generalizable framework for autonomous optimization of biocatalytic cascades and metabolic pathways with direct relevance to industrial biocatalysis and synthetic biology pipelines.

Fourth, from Iqra Ilyas and colleagues with Suowen Xu at the University of Science and Technology of China, in a multi-institution collaboration spanning Bordeaux, Munich, Utrecht, Sheffield, Bad Nauheim, Charlottesville and Shanghai, desert hedgehog — a canonical ligand of the hedgehog signaling pathway — identified as a novel intrinsic endothelial resilience factor that protects against atherosclerosis. Most current cardiovascular pharmacotherapies target risk factors that drive disease rather than factors that protect endothelium in the face of those risk factors. Single-cell sequencing of atheroprone and atheroprotective regions of mouse aorta identified desert hedgehog as a mechanoresponsive gene enriched in regions exposed to unidirectional laminar flow, with expression depleted in atherosclerotic arteries and patient serum. Endothelial-specific knockout aggravated atherosclerosis through enhanced endothelial-mesenchymal transition. Mechanistically, desert hedgehog directly binds the receptor L-R-P-one and competitively blocks its interaction with the protease inhibitor P-A-I-one, suppressing downstream T-G-F-beta and A-K-T and E-R-K signaling that drives endothelial-mesenchymal transition. Recombinant desert hedgehog protein and the P-A-I-one inhibitor T-M-five-two-seven-five each reduced atherosclerosis in ApoE knockout mice — a translatable resilience-based pharmacology distinct from lipid-lowering and antihypertensive standard of care.

That's the brief. Four reasons today to be optimistic about a first-in-class pan-herpesvirus small-molecule modality against the U-L-twelve alkaline nuclease, organ-targeted messenger R-N-A nanoparticle gene replacement that reverses rather than slows polycystic kidney disease, autonomous agentic-A-I-driven enzyme-cascade optimization at industrial scale, and resilience pharmacology of the endothelium against atherosclerosis through the desert-hedgehog and P-A-I-one axis. Until tomorrow.
