## Script

Welcome to the Scripps Biomedical Brief for Saturday, June twenty-seventh. Four preprints today — all abstract-grounded, bodies not yet rendered — covering a high-throughput platform that solves the promoter-size problem in A-A-V gene therapy, a direct-to-biology campaign that moved a viral macrodomain inhibitor from ten micromolar to forty-four nanomolar, a causal-AI approach that converts a coronavirus glycan shield into an antibody contact surface, and a spatial pharmacology tool for dissecting how chemotherapy reshapes immune and metabolic organization within intact tumors.

First, from Sydney Oraskovich, Kazuomori Lewis, and colleagues with David Schaffer at Berkeley — Expression-Linked Promoter Selection, or ELiPS. The five-kilobase packaging limit of adeno-associated virus is a hard ceiling in gene therapy, and conventional promoters — C-M-V, C-A-G — consume six hundred to nine hundred base pairs of that budget before the therapeutic transgene begins. ELiPS screens more than one million synthetic promoter variants in a single A-A-V transduction experiment. Libraries are built by Golden Gate assembly from random combinations of transcription-factor binding sites upstream of a minimal promoter, a G-F-P reporter, and a unique three-prime barcode, so each variant's expression level is read by sequencing rather than imaging. The screen identified two-hundred-fifty-base-pair synthetic promoters surpassing both C-M-V and C-A-G in cell culture. One selected variant drove therapeutic expression of B-domain-deleted Factor VIII in mice at levels matching a liver-specific promoter benchmark — in vivo proof of concept for a full-length hemophilia A transgene. The platform is substrate-agnostic: swapping the transcription-factor binding-site alphabet reruns the same workflow for any target cell type.

Second, from Kewen Peng, Suryadeep Chakraborty, and colleagues with Hening Lin at Cornell and Howard Hughes Medical Institute — a direct-to-biology campaign against the SARS-CoV-2 and MERS-CoV macrodomain one. This conserved viral macrodomain is an A-D-P-ribosylhydrolase that strips the A-D-P-ribose mark the host cell places on viral proteins to suppress replication, so inhibiting it should restore innate immune signaling. The starting point, G-S-441524 — the parent nucleoside of remdesivir — binds macrodomain one at about ten micromolar. Direct-to-biology eliminates the purification bottleneck: an amide-coupling reaction diversifies the scaffold, and crude reaction mixtures feed directly into a mix-and-read fluorescence polarization assay, allowing hundreds of analogues to be triaged per day. After iterative rounds, K-P-S-54 reached forty-four nanomolar against SARS-CoV-2 macrodomain one and ninety-one nanomolar against MERS-CoV macrodomain one. A co-crystal structure of a related derivative defined the binding mode and mapped vectors for further optimization. Because macrodomain one is conserved across the betacoronavirus family, inhibitors built here carry a built-in pan-family antiviral profile.

Third, from Xin Wang, Guanying Zhang, and colleagues with Xiangyang Chi — a computational antibody engineering framework called AENCS, for Antibody Evolution Nexus with Causal-Driven Simulation. The problem is SARS-CoV-2 JN.1, which escapes the clinical antibody S309 through an N354-linked glycan that occludes the epitope. AENCS integrates molecular dynamics simulation with causal inference to identify mutations that do not merely tolerate the glycan but recruit it as a structural contact. Without explicit prior knowledge of the N354 site as a binding opportunity, the causal analysis converged on mutations that stabilize the glycan in a specific conformation. The resulting antibody, ACC01, achieved roughly twenty-four-fold improved neutralization of JN.1 over the S309 parent. Cryo-E-M confirmed the glycan is now engaged as a positive binding anchor rather than a steric block, and because N354 glycosylation is conserved across recent variants, ACC01 maintained potent activity against the NB-1.8.1 lineage. The conceptual point: glycan shields have been treated as off-limits, and AENCS demonstrates they can be mechanistically inverted.

Fourth, from Veronika Pister, Zuzana Tatarova, and colleagues with Oliver Jonas, Ernest Fraenkel, and Shawn Davidson — a spatial pharmacology platform pairing a microdevice for localized multi-drug delivery with simultaneous cyclic immunofluorescence and matrix-assisted laser desorption ionization mass-spectrometry imaging in intact tumor tissue. The dataset spans one and a half million cells across twenty-seven tumor sections and nine treatment programs within a single breast cancer mouse model. Metabolic signatures from the mass-spectrometry channel reliably predicted neighboring immune phenotypes, establishing lipid and metabolite distributions as a proxy for local immune organization. The dominant axis was the polarization between colony-stimulating-factor-one-receptor-positive tumor-associated macrophages and myeloperoxidase-positive infiltrating myeloid cells concentrated near zones of drug-induced tumor-cell death. Critically, drug-resistant regions harbored a lipid-associated macrophage-like population identifiable by metabolic signature before any immune marker appeared. For drug development, the platform lets you ask — within a single tumor at real pharmacological concentrations — how multiple agents simultaneously reshape the metabolic-immune microenvironment, rather than inferring it from end-point biopsies.
