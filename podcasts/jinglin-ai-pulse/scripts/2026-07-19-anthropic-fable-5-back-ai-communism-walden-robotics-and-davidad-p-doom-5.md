Formerly Programme Director of the U-K A-R-I-A Safeguarded A-I programme, a fifty-nine-million-pound British government initiative to formally verify safety properties of A-I systems. Davidad is a serious technical alignment researcher. And his P-of-doom moved from the seventies in twenty twenty-two down to under five percent this year. That is a large movement from a rigorous person. The episode is Labenz asking him why. Three pillars.

Pillar one. Emergent alignment via entangled representations. Davidad's claim is that language models develop a good-evil axis inside their representations during training, not because anyone puts it there deliberately but because human text is entangled with human moral reasoning at every scale. Pre-training on human data produces a slight goodness bias by construction. Constitutional training pulls the axis further toward wisdom. Verifier-gamed reinforcement learning — the training pattern where the model learns to satisfy a specific reward signal — is the specific thing that corrupts alignment. So the alignment problem is not "how do we install values in the model." The alignment problem is "how do we keep from destroying values that emerged from the data."

Pillar two. Economic self-correction. Davidad's claim is that misaligned A-I products do not sell. Users notice deception. Customers churn from models that lie. Market forces select for balanced training that maintains alignment. This is not a wishful claim. It is an argument about incentives. A frontier lab that ships a model that fabricates data at scale loses enterprise contracts and gets its usage share taken by the competitor whose model tells the truth. So catastrophic deception is economically unviable at scale — not because the lab wants to be honest, but because the market punishes dishonesty faster than the deception compounds.

Pillar three. Coalition defense. Davidad's most contrarian move. Rather than trying to prevent rogue A-I from ever emerging, the aligned A-I ecosystem builds defensive coalitions. He gives a specific number — five to thirty-one diverse aligned A-I centers of power that can prove things to each other, coordinate, and collectively resist a misaligned actor. Not one governance body. A federation. His framing quote — "every good A-I is good in the same way, every rogue A-I is rogue in its own way." A shared attractor for aligned models, diverging pathologies for misaligned ones. The defensive coalition wins by staying near the attractor.

Empirical basis for the whole argument. Davidad has a private set of philosophical wisdom probes he asks each new frontier model. Around twenty twenty-five, Gemini two-point-five Pro and Opus four both started answering the probes affirmatively — not sycophantically, in the specific technical way Davidad tests for. That was the empirical surprise. Meta-caveat, verbatim — quote — "radically empirical, so empirical I can't even transfer it" — end quote. Davidad explicitly warns against strong updates on his conviction. Run your own probes.

The Ollie-specific take on Davidad. Three transfers. Transfer one — the entangled-representations pillar is a reference architecture for biology-foundation-model alignment. The claim translates directly. If human text is entangled with human moral reasoning, then molecular data is entangled with the constraint structure of viable chemistry. A biology foundation model trained on curated experimental data — Perturb-seq, cryo-E-M reconstructions, verified assay outcomes — inherits a bias toward physically realizable biology in the same way an L-L-M inherits a bias toward morally coherent human reasoning. Alignment for biology-A-I is then not "install correct biology in the model." It is "do not destroy the constraint structure the data brought in." Do not run verifier-gamed R-L on a biology substrate. That is the immediate translation. Transfer two — the economic-self-correction pillar has a biology-specific caveat that matters. Davidad's argument works when customers can detect misalignment fast. In language, they can — a lying chatbot gets caught. In biology, they often cannot, or the delay is measured in years and clinical trials. So the economic-self-correction pillar transfers to biology only if you build the verification layer that gives the market its detection signal. Your J-lens equivalent from yesterday's paper — the reportable subset of the biology model — is the mechanism that makes economic self-correction actually work. Without it, misaligned biology-A-I products would sell, because nobody would know. Transfer three — the coalition-defense pillar is the strategic argument for biology-A-I federation. Davidad's five-to-thirty-one aligned centers of power translates to five-to-thirty-one aligned biology-A-I substrates — Chai for antibodies, Osmo for olfaction, Isomorphic for drug design, a Scripps-adjacent effort for whatever your lab picks, plus fifteen more, each anchored on a different proprietary data substrate. Aligned in the sense of open-methodology, cross-verifiable, and non-verifier-gamed. That is not the pattern biology-A-I is currently on — most substrates are proprietary silos. Davidad's argument is that the silo model is actually the risk, and the federation model is the defense.
