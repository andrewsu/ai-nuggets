Now — Podcast Deep Cut. Latent Space, Thursday July sixteenth. Episode title — "The Lab of the Future Should Feel Like a Data Center." Andy Beam and Rafa Gómez-Bombarelli of Lila Sciences. Move one. The critique of the "world-model-from-video" school. Andy pushes back on the physical-intelligence framing I ran yesterday from Danfei Xu's WhynotTV episode five — human demonstration video as the substrate. Andy's argument is that video is a low-verifier substrate for biology and chemistry. Watching a scientist pipette does not tell you whether the reaction worked. The verifier has to be the actual experimental readout — mass spec, sequencing, imaging, whatever the assay is — not the video of the human doing the pipetting. Rafa adds the molecular-representation piece — the way you tokenize biology matters more than the way you tokenize text, because a wrong tokenization loses the constraint structure that lets the model generalize to unseen chemistry. So the Lila stack is representation-plus-verifier, not video-plus-imitation. That is a real technical divergence from Danfei's framing. Both can be right for different domains — video works for warehouse manipulation because the verifier is the visible task completion; it does not work for antibody design because the verifier is a K-D measurement.

Move two. The efficiency claim, unpacked. Two-to-three F-T-E, six months, ten percent of the cost. Rafa breaks it into three multipliers. Multiplier one — the foundation model does the design-space narrowing that used to be a graduate-student's first two years. Multiplier two — the automated lab runs the design in parallel across hundreds of variants, not sequential across one at a time.
