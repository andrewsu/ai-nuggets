## Context (not spoken)

### The Nature ac4C paper — what it shows

- **The industry standard problem.** N1-methylpseudouridine (m1Ψ) is the modified nucleotide baked into every FDA-authorized mRNA vaccine and every clinical-stage mRNA therapeutic — the Karikó-Weissman chemistry that unlocked the mRNA field. But two recent Nature papers (Mulroney et al. *Nature* 625:189-194, 2024; Rozman et al. *Nature* 651:533-541, 2026) documented that m1Ψ causes +1 ribosomal frameshifting at U-rich sequences. The frameshift produces truncated / neoantigenic peptides, and immune responses to those frameshifted peptides have been detected in serum from mRNA-COVID-vaccine recipients. m1Ψ is compromised on translational fidelity in a way that is real, mechanistic, and clinically detectable. The field needs an alternative.

- **The chemistry.** ac4C = N4-acetylcytidine. Naturally occurring RNA modification. Oberdoerffer's group previously showed ac4C enhances IVT mRNA translation in HeLa (Arango et al. *Cell* 175:1872-1886, 2018 and subsequent work). Mechanism: ac4C stabilizes Watson-Crick base pairing with G, strengthens tRNA-mRNA decoding in coding sequence, promotes RNA secondary structure. Position-dependent — in coding sequence ac4C promotes elongation via improved cognate-tRNA decoding; in 5'UTR ac4C inhibits initiation via secondary-structure blockade. Cytidine modification, so inherently excluded from start (ATG) and stop codons. The current paper is the head-to-head against m1Ψ in the mRNA-therapeutic setting.

- **The comparison.** IVT NanoLuc + FLuc reporter mRNAs synthesized with ac4C, m1Ψ, m5C (5-methylcytidine), Ψ, or unmodified NTPs. 5' UTRs designed C-less to restrict ac4C to the CDS. Co-transcriptionally capped and polyadenylated. Encapsulated in LNPs.

- **Key finding 1 — protein yield.** In HeLa, THP-1 macrophages, primary human monocyte-derived dendritic cells (MoDCs) from multiple donors, mouse embryonic fibroblasts, and mouse liver in vivo, ac4C-modified mRNA produced higher protein output than m1Ψ, despite comparable transfection and mRNA stability. Extended data figures show fold-increase in hepatic luminescence for ac4C vs m1Ψ at 24h post IV LNP injection in BALB/c mice.

- **Key finding 2 — immunogenicity parity.** ac4C suppressed IFNB1 induction, TNF, and p-eIF2α phosphorylation in immune cells as effectively as m1Ψ, with slightly better numbers in some MoDC donors. Both modifications equally reduced innate immune activation relative to unmodified mRNA. The concern that ac4C might be more immunostimulatory does not survive the multi-donor MoDC data.

- **Key finding 3 — elongation rate.** Live-cell single-molecule imaging of nascent-peptide labeling on individual translating mRNAs (SINAPs), with harringtonine ribosome-runoff kinetics: unmodified runoff median 3.7 min, m5C 4.2 min, ac4C 6.7 min, **m1Ψ 13.7 min**. m1Ψ elongation is ~2x slower than ac4C. This is the mechanistic explanation for the paradox that m1Ψ mRNAs have high ribosome occupancy (heavy polysomes) but low protein output — the ribosomes stall.

- **Key finding 4 — ribosome collisions and RQC.** ZNF598 (ribosome-collision E3 ligase) is recruited to m1Ψ-modified translation sites at much higher rates than ac4C. m1Ψ stalling drives ribosome collisions, which activate RQC (ribosome-associated quality control), which triggers ribosomal subunit splitting and nascent-peptide degradation — the direct throughput cost of the slow elongation.

- **Key finding 5 — fidelity.** Custom N-Flag-FLuc + WT-5×U vs +1FS-5×U (and 5×C variants) reporters. In rabbit reticulocyte lysates: +1FS-5×U produces full-length FLuc only from m1Ψ-modified mRNA (frameshifting). ac4C, m5C, and unmodified fail to produce the frameshift. Double-modification with m1Ψ restores frameshifting. Confirms Mulroney and Rozman results in a clean side-by-side and adds ac4C as a fidelity-preserving alternative. In HeLa cells post-transfection, same result — m1Ψ frameshifts, ac4C doesn't. RiboSeq shows ribosome density accumulating at the U-rich motif with m1Ψ but not ac4C.

- **The scale of the finding.** This is the first head-to-head demonstration that a viable, orthogonal RNA modification chemistry beats the industry standard on both yield and fidelity, in primary immune cells and in mouse liver. Every mRNA therapeutic in clinical development — Moderna, BioNTech, Sanofi/Translate Bio, CureVac, Arcturus, Precision Biosciences, and every liver-tropic LNP program from Alnylam-adjacent players — has a decision to make about m1Ψ replacement. The frameshift-neoantigen issue is real, published in Nature twice, detected in vaccine-recipient serum. Regulatory posture on the frameshift issue is not settled but the field-level answer is "we need the alternative that beats m1Ψ." Nature 2026-07-01 delivers it.
