## Context (not spoken)

### Lieber mPFC-LC paper
- Rodent continuous performance test (rCPT) is a translational sustained-attention paradigm cross-mapped to the human CPT — touch-screen go/no-go with target vs. non-target shapes, parameterized for stimulus duration, ITI, and response window. The translational claim is that rCPT performance metrics (d', responsivity, accuracy, false alarms) are aligned with human CPT metrics used in ADHD, schizophrenia-cognitive-symptom, Alzheimer-attention, and mood-disorder neurocognitive batteries.
- Mechanism dissected: mPFC neurons project to LC (the brain's primary noradrenergic source). Selective chemogenetic activation of mPFC-LC projector neurons (DREADD targeted via projection-defined intersectional viral approach) improves rCPT performance by *enhancing discrimination* (d' increase) — i.e. the cognitive-precision axis. By contrast, non-selective activation of all mPFC neurons improves performance by *increasing responsivity* (more hits, more false alarms, no d' improvement). Two functionally distinct mPFC subpopulations engage non-overlapping downstream targets and control different attention domains.
- Downstream microcircuit: mPFC-LC projector activation recruits LC-NE neurons plus glutamatergic and GABAergic peri-LC neurons; net effect is an increase in noradrenergic tone within the mPFC itself (closed-loop). Non-selective mPFC activation also recruits nucleus accumbens (NAc) targets, which is why it produces a "more responding" phenotype rather than a "more accurate" phenotype.
- Therapeutic framing from the paper (verbatim from abstract): "providing a circuit-level framework for understanding the mechanisms of sustained attention and for developing targeted therapies for attentional deficits across neuropsychiatric disorders." Authors explicitly flag cross-indication potential — ADHD, schizophrenia cognitive symptoms, age-related attention decline.
- Lieber-platform read: Carr's PPT / Lieber dual appointment means this is part of a Lieber-led neuropsychiatric circuit/drug-discovery pipeline, not an academic curiosity. Pergola published two Lieber infrastructure assets earlier this month — trans-TWAS INGENE/MODULE target-discovery engine (covered Thursday) and the sign-tracker/goal-tracker D2 biomarker (Friday). This is the third Lieber asset in three weeks on the "platform-layer infrastructure" reading — and the only one of the three that opens a non-dopaminergic CNS target lane.
- Competitive landscape (NE/attention/CNS): Otsuka/Sunovion has the established noradrenergic ADHD market (Strattera/atomoxetine, NRI); Supernus has SPN-812 / viloxazine in adolescents/adults; Aevi/Cingulate explored selective D1 positive modulators; CytoDyn's leronlimab and others moved off the attention indication. The Lieber paper is *not* a drug — it's a circuit-target story that identifies a closed-loop NE-amplification axis for which selective LC-projection-defined targeting (rather than systemic NE blanket) could give the discrimination-axis improvement without the wakefulness / cardiovascular liabilities of broad NE modulators. Conceptually parallel to the Mysore PLTi work covered earlier this month (anatomically-defined attention target) but with a Lieber/JHU pedigree and an in-house medicinal chemistry handoff.

### JHU Segars/Borahay/Elisseeff uterine fibroid paper
- Uterine fibroids: ~70% lifetime prevalence in women, drives heavy menstrual bleeding, pain, infertility, hysterectomy (~600k/yr in the US alone). Market is grossly under-served therapeutically — current standard is GnRH-antagonist add-back combos (Oriahnn/AbbVie elagolix; Myfembree/Pfizer-Sumitomo relugolix-E2-NETA; Yselty/Gedeon Richter linzagolix). All three are symptom-control hormonal therapies with finite-duration use due to bone-density warnings. No FDA-approved disease-modifying agent on the fibrosis biology.
- Mechanism in this paper: substrate-stiffness-driven senescence in fibroid cells is sufficient to upregulate fibrotic markers (fibronectin, PAI-1, αSMA) plus senescence markers (p16, p21). Src kinase + β-catenin are identified as the regulatory nodes. Pharmacology readouts: dasatinib (approved BCR-ABL inhibitor, also a Src inhibitor) reduces both senescence and fibrosis markers and attenuates β-catenin activity, positioning Src upstream of β-catenin. β-catenin inhibitors ICG-001 and PRI-724 (Prism BioLab / Eisai-derived chemistry) suppress senescence + fibrosis markers and reduce CTGF and cyclin D1 downstream. Mechanistic link from biomechanics (matrix stiffness) → cellular senescence → tissue fibrosis.
- Borahay/Segars are part of the JHU Drug Discovery Program adjacent to Slusher; Elisseeff is the TTEC director and a serial spinout founder (Aegeria Soft Tissue, ZEISS-related). Phillip BME at JHU contributes mechanotransduction methodology. The corresponding author is Segars.
- Commercial caveat: dasatinib repurposing in fibroids is open territory (no IP barrier on the molecule itself), but the *senolytic-in-fibroids* indication is a positionable use-patent angle if first-mover. β-catenin small molecules (ICG-001, PRI-724) have been in clinical trials in oncology / fibrosis with mixed results — well-trodden chemistry, less defensible IP. The IP question is around either (a) novel Src-selective or β-catenin-pathway molecules tuned for the fibroid tissue context, or (b) the mechanism-based combination of senolytic + anti-fibrotic small molecules in a uterine-specific delivery vehicle. Sci Reports is a lower-tier journal for the work, which suggests the team's flagship paper is elsewhere — worth checking whether the Borahay lab is preparing a follow-up Nature Medicine / Cell Reports Medicine submission with the in vivo / preclinical pharmacology readouts.

## Script

Good morning, Matt — this is Sourcing Radar for Sunday, June twenty-eighth. Short one today because Portfolio Watch follows. Two picks.

The lead is a Lieber Institute paper out Friday in Neuropsychopharmacology — Jorge Miranda-Barrientos corresponding, Keri Martinowich and Greg Carr senior co-authors, all three dual-appointed Lieber plus Johns Hopkins. The title is sustained-attention top-down control by medial-prefrontal-to-locus-coeruleus projection neurons in the rodent continuous performance test. Let me walk it.

The rodent continuous performance test is a translational attention paradigm cross-mapped to the same touch-screen go/no-go that's used in human attention batteries for A-D-H-D, schizophrenia cognitive symptoms, Alzheimer attention, and mood-disorder neurocognition. Translational alignment is the entire point of the assay.

What the paper does is dissect the prefrontal cortex into two functionally distinct subpopulations using a projection-defined chemogenetic approach. The medial prefrontal cortex projects to the locus coeruleus, which is the brain's primary source of norepinephrine. Selectively activating just the prefrontal-to-locus-coeruleus projector neurons improves attention performance by raising discriminability — d-prime increases — meaning the animals get more accurate, not just more responsive. Activating the entire prefrontal cortex non-selectively also improves performance, but by a completely different mechanism — animals respond more, hit more targets, but also make more false alarms, with no discrimination improvement. Two prefrontal subpopulations, two functional axes of attention, non-overlapping downstream targets. That is a cleanly dissociated cognitive geometry.

The microcircuit is the elegant part. Activating prefrontal-to-locus-coeruleus projectors recruits not only the locus coeruleus norepinephrine neurons themselves but also glutamatergic and GABAergic peri-locus-coeruleus neurons in the surrounding region. Net effect is a closed-loop increase in norepinephrine tone back inside the prefrontal cortex. The non-selective prefrontal activation engages nucleus accumbens targets instead, which is why it produces a more-responding phenotype rather than a more-accurate phenotype. Anatomy maps to cognitive domain.

Three things this means for Blackbird.

First — the strategic frame for the G-P-R fifty-two program with Lieber and Third Rock. The G-P-R fifty-two thesis is built on hitting all three symptom domains of schizophrenia — positive, negative, and cognitive — without dopamine. Cognitive symptoms in schizophrenia are anchored in attention and working-memory deficits. That is a cognitive-domain story that does not compete with G-P-R fifty-two, it sits alongside it. Now the second pick. A Sci Reports paper out Friday from the Borahay-Segars group at Johns Hopkins Reproductive Sciences with Jennifer Elisseeff at the Translational Tissue Engineering Center — uterine fibroid biology, S-R-C and beta-catenin as a senescence-fibrosis axis driven by matrix stiffness. Brief, because it isn't the flagship paper of the program — it's a mechanism paper that points at what the program could be.

Two things to know about the uterine-fibroid market. One — it is enormous and under-served. Fibroids hit roughly seventy percent of women by menopause, drive most of the six hundred thousand hysterectomies done in the US each year, and the only F-D-A-approved options are three G-n-R-H-antagonist combinations from AbbVie, Pfizer-Sumitomo, and Gedeon Richter that are symptom-control with finite duration because of bone density warnings. There is no approved disease-modifying agent on the fibrosis biology itself. Two — the biology of fibroids is mechanotransduction-plus-senescence-plus-fibrosis, and that is the lane this paper opens.

The mechanism this paper shows is that matrix stiffness alone drives both senescence — p-sixteen, p-twenty-one — and fibrotic markers — fibronectin, P-A-I-one, alpha-S-M-A — in fibroid cells, and that the regulatory nodes are S-R-C kinase upstream and beta-catenin downstream. Dasatinib, the approved B-C-R-A-B-L drug that also inhibits S-R-C, attenuates both senescence and fibrosis and shuts down beta-catenin. Two beta-catenin inhibitors — I-C-G-zero-zero-one and P-R-I-seven-twenty-four — phenocopy the dasatinib effect at the senescence and fibrosis readouts and downregulate C-T-G-F and cyclin-D-one. So the mechanism logic is biomechanics drives senescence, senescence drives fibrosis, S-R-C-and-beta-catenin is the druggable node.

The Blackbird angle is forward-looking. The molecules in this paper are repurposed legacy assets — dasatinib has no composition-of-matter advantage here, and beta-catenin inhibitors as a class have been clinically explored. What is patentable is either a novel Src-selective or beta-catenin-pathway chemistry tuned for uterine tissue context, or a use-patent on the senolytic-plus-anti-fibrotic combination concept in fibroids.
