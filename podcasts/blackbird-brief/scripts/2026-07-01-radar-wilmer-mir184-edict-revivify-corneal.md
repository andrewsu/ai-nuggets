### What the miR-184 paper shows

- miR-184 is the most abundant miRNA in the corneal epithelium, lens, and retina — a canonical developmental and homeostatic ocular miRNA. The +57C>T seed-region mutation is one of the first miRNAs implicated in a Mendelian disease: EDICT syndrome (endothelial dystrophy, iris hypoplasia, congenital cataract, and stromal thinning), autosomal dominant. Prior work has mapped the family history and rough phenotype; this is the first paper to combine multi-generational quantitative biometry + iPSC-derived corneal endothelial cell (CEC) mechanistic readouts.
- **Family cohort:** 18 members of a four-generation family. 10/18 carriers heterozygous for +57C>T; **5/10 carriers had retinal detachment vs 0/8 non-carriers** (p=0.04). This dramatically expands the phenotype beyond corneal biology to a systemic anterior-segment + retinal-attachment disease. Retinal detachment in a genetically-defined AD family with 50% penetrance is a big deal — surgical/anatomical intervention only, no medical therapy for the underlying susceptibility.
- **Quantitative biometry (carriers vs non-carriers, age+sex adjusted):**
  - Axial length **-2.2 mm** (p=0.02) — microphthalmia
  - Mean keratometry **+9.3 D steeper** (p=0.004) — high corneal curvature
  - Horizontal corneal diameter **-1.6 mm** (p=0.0001) — microcornea
  - Central corneal thickness **-139 μm** (p=0.003) — corneal thinning
  - These are not subtle. This is a fully quantifiable ocular developmental phenotype anchored to a single miRNA seed-region base change.
- **Mechanistic iPSC-CEC:** Patient iPSCs differentiated into corneal endothelial cells recapitulate the disease at the cellular level — irregular cell borders, increased cell + nucleus area, widened intercellular gaps, disrupted ATP1A1 (Na+/K+ ATPase pump) membrane localization, and reduced barrier function on transendothelial electrical resistance (TEER). Gene expression: **COL4A1↓, COL4A3↓, AQP1↓, COL8A1↑** — extracellular matrix + pump-gene dysregulation.
- Mechanism reading: a seed-region SNV (+57C>T) alters the target repertoire of miR-184, dysregulating the ECM + pump programs that keep the corneal endothelium as a functional pump-barrier monolayer. The developmental biometric phenotype implicates miR-184 in coordinated anterior-posterior eye morphogenesis; the CEC phenotype implicates miR-184 in postnatal corneal endothelial homeostasis.

### The BChE paper (second pick)

- **Michael Betenbaugh** — JHU Department of Chemical & Biomolecular Engineering, 30+ years of mammalian cell-line engineering. This is a peer-reviewed Cell Systems paper on the **glycoform engineering of a mammalian platform** for **recombinant human butyrylcholinesterase (rHuBChE)**.
- **What they did:** Compared HEK293, CHO, and HepG2 platforms for producing rHuBChE with a native human plasma-like glycan profile. **HEK293 was the most promising baseline platform.** Then extensive glycoengineering — knocking out and modulating >10 glycosylation-related genes — to (a) knock out fucosylation, (b) enhance sialylation, (c) reduce glycan branching. Result: near-native plasma-BChE glycoprofile.
- **Why glycan profile matters for BChE:** Native plasma-derived HuBChE has an unusually high sialic-acid + tetraantennary glycan burden; those glycans are critical for the >70-day plasma half-life that makes BChE useful as a stoichiometric bioscavenger. Recombinant BChE from wild-type HEK or CHO fails clinically because the glycan mismatch collapses the half-life to hours and drives immunogenicity. This is why the plasma-derived approach (Baxter Nexterone / Baxalta / earlier programs) has been the field's only viable path, at limited production capacity.
- **Market pull:** OP nerve agent + pesticide exposure. Bioscavengers are stoichiometric — you need moles of enzyme per mole of OP; a dose is grams of protein. This is a US DoD / BARDA / BioShield Special Reserve Fund market — lumpy, government-driven, non-VC-friendly for the direct product but valuable if the platform is the asset. Historical programs: PharmAthene / Kamada / BioTherapeutics rHuBChE, ProMetic Life Sciences plasma-derived HuBChE (Ravasco), all stalled at scale-up cost.
- **The Blackbird-relevant frame is the platform, not the asset.** A HEK293 mammalian cell line with 10+ orthogonal glycoengineering edits that hits a matched native plasma glycoprofile is a **general biosimilar + high-value-glycoprotein production platform**. Applications: (a) enzyme replacement therapies (Fabry α-Gal-A, Gaucher β-glucocerebrosidase, Pompe α-glucosidase — where glycan-directed cellular uptake and half-life matter), (b) glycan-engineered antibodies (afucosylated / bisected structures for enhanced ADCC), (c) coagulation factors + fusion proteins where glycan-driven PK is the differentiator.
- **JHTV connection:** Betenbaugh's group has a long JHU tenure but only sparse commercial handoffs (his cell-line engineering IP has historically been consumed inside collaborations with industrial partners like AstraZeneca, MilliporeSigma). This paper is a strong candidate for a **platform-out spin from the ChemBE + JHTV axis**. Diligence question is whether Betenbaugh is at the "shopping" stage or already inside a strategic collaboration that would preclude a new spin.
