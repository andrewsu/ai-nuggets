### 1. Roche divarasib Krascendo-1 beats Lumakras + Krazati head-to-head

- **The data.** In previously-treated KRAS G12C non-small-cell lung cancer, oral divarasib delivered statistically significant overall-survival and progression-free-survival wins over both Amgen's Lumakras (sotorasib) and BMS's Krazati (adagrasib) in the same trial — the first Phase 3 head-to-head between a next-gen KRAS G12C inhibitor and the two approved competitors. Safety consistent with prior divarasib data.
- **What it means for the KRAS field.** Divarasib is the "next-gen" G12C inhibitor Roche has been building around Kraas — higher selectivity, deeper responses, cleaner adverse-event profile. A Phase 3 head-to-head win over both approved competitors resets the standard-of-care ceiling for KRAS G12C NSCLC. Roche's next-catalyst readout is Krascendo-2 (divarasib + Keytruda first-line), which is now the $6B question for the class. Amgen and BMS both need next-generation follow-ons or combination strategies.
- **Blackbird portfolio touch — aSKY.** aSKY Therapeutics sits in the KRAS-adjacent oncology space (UMB Krupnick lab; AVS Bio partnership on antibody engineering with a KRAS frame, per LinkedIn late 2025). aSKY's disclosed MOA remains not-formally-public. Reading the divarasib result — this is a **validation with a caveat** for KRAS-adjacent programs. On the validation side: KRAS-directed oncology remains a live differentiable class in the clinic, big-pharma appetite for KRAS assets is intact, and Roche is running the offensive playbook. On the caveat side: the ceiling for direct G12C TKI approaches just got higher. Any KRAS-adjacent program should be positioned as *complementary to* rather than *competing with* the next-gen TKI class — combination synergy, resistance-mechanism coverage, or non-G12C-mutant tumors. Krupnick's lung-cancer NK-cell biology background is naturally an immune-adjacency story, not a TKI story, which is where aSKY's positioning holds up cleanly.

### 2. Abivax obefazimod Phase 3 malignancy signal cleared

- **The data.** Abivax reported supplementary Phase 3 (ABTECT Part 2) exposure-adjusted cancer analysis on approximately 1,700 patient-years of obefazimod exposure. Cancer rates in obefazimod-treated ulcerative-colitis patients are at background UC-population rates. The signal that earlier spooked investors and depressed the stock is resolved. Abivax is now signaling Crohn's expansion.
- **What obefazimod is mechanistically.** Oral small molecule that upregulates the microRNA miR-124, which downregulates a set of pro-inflammatory genes. That is the *opposite direction* of the standard mechanistic playbook (silence a pathological RNA) — obefazimod *increases* an endogenous regulatory RNA to reset the inflammatory program. It is the most clinically advanced example of the RNA-upregulator small-molecule modality in inflammation.
- **Portfolio touch #1 — Slusher gut-restricted GCPII IBD program.** Threat direction. A cleaner-safety oral small molecule with prevalent-disease commercial ambition — Crohn's plus UC together are a $10B+ market — is now de-risked. This does not kill the GCPII program (GCPII targets a distinct axis, glutamate-carboxypeptidase-II inflammation and enteric-nervous-system pain, with a different mechanism from miR-124/pro-inflammatory-gene modulation) but the ceiling for oral small-molecule IBD entrants just got harder to cross. The Slusher program's differentiation story must be **the pain axis** — GCPII's bimodal effect on inflammation *and* enteric neuropathic pain is the differentiation obefazimod does not touch. Pain is real and enormous unmet need in IBD; that is where the pitch defends.
- **Portfolio touch #2 — RNA translation-activator platform.** Validation direction. Abivax cleared safety on a Phase 3 RNA-modulating small molecule that *upregulates* a target — that is the closest thing to a live proof point that the "increase, not silence" philosophy can produce a pill-form blockbuster in a prevalent inflammatory disease. Blackbird's translation-activator platform sits in the same conceptual neighborhood: small-molecule modulation of RNA to *boost* target protein output. The mechanistic direction is different (obefazimod = miRNA induction to suppress inflammation; Blackbird platform = translation activation to raise haploinsufficient protein levels), but the *category* is the same — RNA-directed small molecules that increase rather than silence. Useful tailwind for Avi when the Blackbird translation-activator program is positioned to outside investors. Camp4 competitive validation as well.
- **Action items.** Avi — the Abivax ABTECT result is a citable market-validation data point for the Blackbird translation-activator platform's next outside pitch. Slusher / gut-GCPII team — the pain-axis differentiation story needs to be sharpened before this hits BD conversations, because the ceiling for pure-inflammation IBD oral small molecules just moved up.
