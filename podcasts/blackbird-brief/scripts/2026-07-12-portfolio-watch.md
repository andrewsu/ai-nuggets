# Blackbird Brief — 2026-07-12 — Portfolio Watch

Slug: blackbird-brief. Episode type: Portfolio Watch (Sunday; paired with today's Sourcing Radar episode).

## Script

This is The Blackbird Brief, Portfolio Watch for the week ending Sunday, July 12, 2026. The single most important development for the portfolio this week is not on the Blackbird side of the ledger — it is on the competitive side. Nxera Pharma, the London-headquartered specialty neuroscience company that used to be Sosei Heptares, is actively shopping its Phase-two-ready G-P-R fifty-two agonist program for schizophrenia, following Boehringer Ingelheim's decision in December to walk away from the same asset after paying a substantial upfront to option it. That is a direct competitive-intel item on the Lieber Institute G-P-R fifty-two Neuropsych NewCo, and it is where we start. The rest of the week has real portfolio-relevant news on Aletira's regulatory environment, the Slusher G-C-P-two I-B-D competitive landscape, and a validating read for the R-N-A translation-activator platform — but the Nxera signal is the story of the week.

**Lieber neuropsych — G-P-R fifty-two competitive landscape.** Three items, all interlocking. First, Nxera. Boehringer Ingelheim optioned Nxera's G-P-R fifty-two agonist — the program was originally H-T-L-0016878, has been through Phase one and was reported ready to enter Phase two — with option-take-up expected in the third or fourth quarter of last year. Boehringer instead walked away from the option in December 2025, and Nxera has been shopping the program since. It is the only other Phase-two-ready G-P-R fifty-two agonist in the industry. The Lieber NewCo, by contrast, is still working toward a development candidate declaration expected later this year — meaning if a specialist neuroscience buyer picks up Nxera's program in the third quarter, we may find ourselves twelve to twenty-four months behind a direct same-mechanism competitor at the moment of our own D-C nomination. This shifts the calendar for the Lieber conversation. Two things Eddie and Hemaka should be doing: one, tracking the Nxera bidder set through the third quarter — the specialty neuroscience acquirers with the appetite and the strategic gap are Otsuka, Karuna post-B-M-S integration, Neurocrine, and the reawakening AbbVie-Cerevel unit; and two, making sure the Lieber NewCo differentiation story sharpens now, while there is still a case to be made that our G-P-R fifty-two chemistry is best-in-class rather than just first-Phase-two.

The second item in the same section is Kynexis and K-Y-N-fifty-three-fifty-six, the K-A-T two inhibitor licensed from Mitsubishi Tanabe. Kynexis is a Netherlands-VC-backed company that Robert Schwarcz at U-M-B cofounded — we walked through the human proof-of-mechanism paper from Schwarcz and Buchanan two days ago on the radar side, in the tryptophan-challenge trial. The public disclosure is that the Kynexis Phase two schizophrenia cognitive impairment trial reads out topline in the fourth quarter of this year. Also ahead of the Lieber NewCo D-C. If K-Y-N-fifty-three-fifty-six works in Phase two on cognition, the "all three symptom domains" narrative that G-P-R fifty-two is being positioned around gets its cognition beat validated by a competitor first. Not a threat to the mechanism — the mechanisms are complementary and both could serve the same patient — but a threat to the pole-position framing.

The third item, and it is a threat-softening item, is Cobenfy. B-M-S paused enrollment in the ADEPT-two trial of Cobenfy in Alzheimer's disease psychosis — this is the muscarinic Karuna asset now inside B-M-S — after B-M-S disclosed enrollment-integrity issues at a subset of trial sites. The trial is being extended with additional enrollment; the full ADEPT read-out that was tracking to earlier this year now stacks into the end of 2026. The read for the Lieber NewCo is that the near-term muscarinic-class share-of-voice compression in cognition and indication-expansion narratives just got pushed back six to nine months. That is a modest tailwind for our launch positioning window, though it does not resolve the underlying issue of Cobenfy already being an approved schizophrenia product.

Net-net on Lieber neuropsych: the calendar has tightened. Nxera getting picked up is the biggest single risk to our positioning, Kynexis reading out on cognition is the second, and the Cobenfy delay is a partial offset. This is a conversation for Matt and Hemaka with our Third Rock counterparts on whether Lieber's D-C timing needs to compress from later 2026 into the third quarter.

**Aletira Therapeutics — regulatory and competitive landscape.** Two items. First, on the regulatory side, the F-D-A formally opened the search for a permanent director of the C-B-E-R Office of Therapeutic Products, applications due July 21. This is the office that reviews A-A-V gene therapies. We covered Vijay Kumar's exit in last week's Portfolio Watch. The formal search opening tells us the interim reporting arrangement — Karim Mikhail as acting C-B-E-R director overseeing directly — is going to last at least through the third quarter, probably into the fourth. For Aletira, whose I-N-D-enabling package is on the horizon, the specific worry is that a permanent-director gap at O-T-P slows or destabilizes the review-culture pivot toward more rare-disease flexibility that Kumar had been steering. Interim leadership tends to move slowly on precedent-setting review positions. This is a watch-item drag, not an existential change. Eddie is tracking the external-candidate short list; the identity of who the F-D-A hires and their prior A-A-V review experience will shape the Aletira review climate for a decade.

Second Aletira item. Regeneron's Otarmeni — that is the O-T-O-F A-A-V gene therapy from the Akouos side of Regeneron's rare-disease franchise — remains the sole approved A-A-V gene therapy for hereditary hearing loss in the United States, following its April 2026 approval under the C-N-P-V pilot pathway. Regeneron continues to sell it under a free-in-U-S commercial model — no reimbursement demand — which is a strategic signal. It essentially sets a zero-dollar price floor for second-in-class O-T-O-F entrants like Sensorion. The commercial read for Aletira is that Aletira's non-O-T-O-F, cell-type-selective positioning is well insulated from this dynamic — Aletira's playbook is not to compete on O-T-O-F, and the target-white-space read on non-O-T-O-F hereditary hearing loss remains open. Sensorion, for its part, has not posted new data in the window since the March six-month Audiogene read, so no new dot on that graph. The competitive read is: Otarmeni is defining the O-T-O-F market floor at zero, which is bad news for Sensorion's monetization narrative and neutral-to-good news for Aletira's non-O-T-O-F differentiation story.

**Slusher G-C-P-two I-B-D — competitive threat landscape.** Two items. Merck delivered a Phase three win with tulisokibart in the ATLAS-U-C induction trial on June 22 — that is spillover from just outside our week window, but it is material because tulisokibart is the first anti-T-L-one-A biologic to hit primary and key-secondary endpoints in a Phase three registrational induction study in moderately to severely active ulcerative colitis. Merck also just initiated three new Phase 2b indications for tulisokibart, staking a T-L-one-A pan-inflammation franchise. Why this matters for Slusher's oral gut-restricted G-C-P-two program: the I-B-D competitive space is being consolidated faster than expected around biologics with prevalent-disease reach — Merck tulisokibart T-L-one-A, Abivax obefazimod oral m-i-R one-twenty-four upregulator now Phase three-clean on the malignancy signal, J-N-J's icotide oral I-C-O-N-I-C-U-C, and the J-N-J Tremfya versus AbbVie Skyrizi head-to-head Crohn's initiating. The differentiation story for Slusher's G-C-P-two needs to be the pain axis — the bimodal effect on inflammation and enteric-nervous-system pain that none of these mechanisms address. The ceiling for a pure-inflammation oral small molecule in I-B-D just moved up meaningfully; if the pitch is not pain, the pitch is not defensible.

Second in the same section: Vera Therapeutics' TRUTAKNA — atacicept — received F-D-A accelerated approval on Tuesday, July 7, for I-g-A nephropathy. That is the first approved dual BAFF plus APRIL neutralizing molecule. Vera did the "mechanism-repurposing off a failed 2010 Merck-Serono lupus asset" playbook that Foresite operates. The reason this matters for the Slusher I-B-D program — indirect but real — is that the autoimmune indication cluster is being drained at an unusual pace. Five I-g-A nephropathy drugs and four mechanisms in about two years — Tarpeyo, Filspari, Fabhalta, Voyxact, and now TRUTAKNA. Autoimmune categories are consolidating around specific mechanistic buckets; the I-B-D bucket has T-L-one-A, I-L-twenty-three, m-i-R-N-A, and integrin-α-four-β-seven already staked. G-C-P-two's differentiation story — enteric neuropathic pain plus inflammation as a bimodal mechanism — has to be crisp enough to survive an autoimmune-market that is compressing timelines and enforcing tighter mechanistic specialization. Not a threat to the underlying biology; a pressure on the positioning.

**R-N-A translation-activator platform — validation.** One item, and it is the strongest single validating datapoint the small-molecule R-N-A modulation category has produced in the year. Skyhawk Therapeutics reported the twelve-month interim from the Phase one two trial of S-K-Y-oh-five-fifteen in Huntington's disease last quarter, and confirmed U-S site expansion into FALCON-H-D Phase two three in the past week. S-K-Y-oh-five-fifteen is an oral, C-N-S-penetrant small-molecule R-N-A splicing modulator that reduces mutant huntingtin. Twelve-month interim data show up to sixty-nine percent mutant huntingtin reduction in patients, with modest clinical improvement on the composite unified Huntington's disease rating scale, and a clean safety profile in more than one hundred seventy-five patients dosed. Skyhawk is now the reference for what "small-molecule R-N-A modulator gets to registrational-scale trial with reasonable durability and safety" actually looks like in the real world. Blackbird's R-N-A translation-activator platform sits in an adjacent quadrant — translation activation rather than splice modulation — but the "small molecule increasing rather than silencing a target R-N-A" thesis is the same category. Avi should have the Skyhawk twelve-month package as a citable proof point in the next outside pitch of the Blackbird translation activator. This is a real tailwind for the modality narrative.

**1104health — regulatory tailwind, briefly.** The F-D-A has moved forward on the real-time clinical trials pilot — R-T-C-T — with formal selection criteria distributed in July, and the first pilot programs already running on the Paradigm Health infrastructure: AstraZeneca's TrAVeRse in mantle cell lymphoma, Amgen's STREAM-S-C-L-C in small-cell lung cancer. The F-D-A endorsing tech-mediated real-time review of trial data is a direct thesis-tailwind for 1104health — Rose Wang's clinician-facing marketplace embedding trial options into community oncology workflow is exactly the kind of tech infrastructure that gets pulled into these R-T-C-T pilots. The competing platform, Massive Bio, has been public with its Reticulum Nexus rollout and an OpenAI partnership announced in April. If Massive Bio wins the first hospital-system contract for R-T-C-T support, that shapes the segment early. Worth Rose being visible to F-D-A on the R-T-C-T selection criteria window and pushing for pilot inclusion.

Wrapping up. The week's biggest story for the portfolio is the Nxera G-P-R fifty-two program shopping in the same quarter that Kynexis is heading into a Phase two K-A-T two readout on cognition — the Lieber NewCo needs to move faster on the D-C conversation and sharpen its differentiation before a direct same-mechanism competitor is picked up by a specialty acquirer. The Aletira regulatory picture is on hold pending a permanent O-T-P director but the non-O-T-O-F white-space is intact. The Slusher G-C-P-two I-B-D positioning needs to keep leading on the pain axis; the biologics are consolidating faster than expected. Skyhawk gave the R-N-A modulator category its cleanest twelve-month data point yet, which we should be citing on the translation-activator side. And 1104health has a real F-D-A-endorsed tailwind through the R-T-C-T pilot channel. Radar comes back tomorrow. This has been The Blackbird Brief.
