## Context (not spoken)

### The BDNF-DT paper — what it shows and why the Lieber connection matters

- **The setup.** The paper is a discovery-and-characterization report on two previously-uncatalogued RNAs at the human BDNF locus, from Daniel Weinberger's group at the Lieber Institute for Brain Development. Weinberger is Lieber's founding director; Kleinman + Hyde + Martinowich are core Lieber senior investigators; Ursini is the paper's senior co-corresponding author. This is a Lieber-anchored fundamental-neurobiology paper in *Molecular Psychiatry*.

- **What they identified.** Re-analysis of postmortem dorsolateral prefrontal cortex (DLPFC) RNA-seq from a large Lieber-generated dataset (Jaffe et al. *Nat Neurosci* 2018) identified poly-A+ reads upstream of BDNF exon I on the antisense strand — reads not annotated in the current Ensembl reference. Validation by end-to-end PCR + cloning + Sanger sequencing on independent DLPFC samples confirmed three main transcripts of a new gene they named **BDNF-DT** (BDNF divergent transcript) — BDNF-DT1, 2, and 3 — plus one **BDNF-AS-DT** readthrough transcript formed by splicing of exons from the previously-known BDNF-AS with the new BDNF-DT. BDNF-AS-DT does **not** overlap the BDNF protein-coding sequence, distinguishing it from BDNF-AS.

- **The regulatory relationship with BDNF.**
  - Developmental profile — BDNF-DT expression increases from prenatal life, decreases in the childhood-to-adolescence transition, peaks again around age 25, then declines. That trajectory **mirrors BDNF**. In contrast, the previously-annotated BDNF-AS antisense transcript increases with adulthood + senescence — the classic antisense pattern.
  - Correlation — BDNF-DT is **positively** correlated with BDNF across development; BDNF-AS-DT is **negatively** correlated with BDNF; BDNF-AS shows **no** correlation with BDNF. So there are opposing regulatory dynamics at the same locus.
  - Activity-dependence — depolarization of cultured human neurons increases BDNF-DT + BDNF-AS-DT expression, and the temporal dynamics follow BDNF's own activity-dependent expression profile — consistent with a regulatory rather than merely divergent relationship.
  - **CRISPR-activation causal test** — CRISPR-mediated upregulation of BDNF in human neural progenitor cells (hNPCs) drives **BDNF-DT expression as a downstream consequence**. This is a functional coupling — you cannot upregulate BDNF without co-upregulating BDNF-DT.

- **The schizophrenia genetics.**
  - Case-control DLPFC — BDNF-DT is **higher** in schizophrenia DLPFC than neurotypical controls.
  - eQTL / imputation — genetically-predicted **lower** BDNF-AS-DT (the readthrough transcript) is associated with schizophrenia risk. And genetically-predicted lower BDNF-AS-DT is associated with the schizophrenia-risk **C** allele of **rs6265** — the classic BDNF Val66Met SNP (Val = C allele; Met = T allele). rs6265 is the most-studied BDNF SNP in psychiatric genetics; it changes activity-dependent BDNF secretion and has been linked to hippocampal function, PTSD, depression, cognition, and schizophrenia in dozens of studies. **This paper offers a molecular mechanism for the rs6265 association via a downstream regulatory RNA — the C allele's schizophrenia risk may be mediated by reduced BDNF-AS-DT expression, not by (or in addition to) the Val→Met protein change itself.**
  - REST binding — the 5' region of BDNF-DT overlaps a REST binding site on the BDNF gene, and co-expression network analysis shows genes co-expressed with BDNF + BDNF-DT are predicted to be regulated by REST (the neuron-restrictive silencer element). REST is the master transcriptional repressor of neural genes; a Huntington's-disease-relevant node (huntingtin binds REST). This positions BDNF-DT in the REST-BDNF regulatory axis that has been studied in HD, depression, and schizophrenia.

- **What this is and isn't.** The paper's own concluding line: "though their biological significance requires further validation given detection limitations and the need to establish causal roles." This is discovery-and-characterization biology, not a target-declaration paper. But the discovery has three specific translational hooks:
  1. **A native BDNF-upregulation regulatory element.** If BDNF-DT positively co-varies with BDNF and is directly driven by BDNF CRISPRa in hNPCs, then any therapy aiming to *upregulate* endogenous BDNF acts through (or alongside) BDNF-DT. A translation-activating or transcription-activating oligo strategy targeting BDNF has to reckon with the divergent-transcript layer — either as an on-target biomarker of BDNF induction or as an ancillary lever.
  2. **A schizophrenia-genetics-linked biomarker.** Reduced BDNF-AS-DT is associated with schizophrenia risk + the rs6265 C allele. In a Lieber-Blackbird schizophrenia NewCo, BDNF-AS-DT is a candidate pharmacodynamic biomarker for target engagement + patient stratification (rs6265-genotyped subgroups).
  3. **A REST-axis handle.** REST is a well-studied but pharmacologically-elusive transcriptional repressor. The BDNF-DT / BDNF-AS-DT axis is a specific instance of REST-dependent regulation with a clinical readout. Mechanistic follow-on can identify small-molecule or oligo strategies acting on this axis.

- **Data + tools.** Data behind dbGaP phs000979.v3.p2 (Lieber DLPFC RNA-seq) + GTEx. No new spinout / IP disclosure; no competing interests declared. The paper is a public-domain-quality contribution to the Lieber schizophrenia thesis stack.
