## Script

Good morning, Justice. Today's item is a Phase 3 readout that closes a decade-old open question in the A-D-C world — does B seven H three actually work as a pivotal-grade target for antibody-drug conjugates. Hansoh Pharma and G-S-K announced yesterday, Friday July tenth, that ARTEMIS-008, the Chinese Phase 3 of risvutatug rezetecan — brand designation Ris-Rez, formerly H-S two-oh-oh-nine-three — met its primary endpoint of overall survival in advanced or relapsed small-cell lung cancer, with a statistically significant and clinically meaningful improvement over topotecan, which is the second-line standard of care. Progression-free survival tracked with the O-S signal. Safety profile in line with prior data, no new signals. The trial was blinded on the sponsor side going in, so the effect size is not yet public — that will land at a medical meeting later this year, presumably W-C-L-C or E-S-M-O Asia. The frame for the announcement is what makes today matter — this is the first-ever positive Phase 3 O-S readout for a B seven H three-targeted A-D-C in any tumor type.

Start with the molecule, because it maps directly onto the payload conversation we had four days ago on the Novartis-Myricx deal. Ris-Rez is a fully human anti-B seven H three monoclonal antibody, tetrapeptide-based cleavable linker, and a topoisomerase-one inhibitor payload — an exatecan derivative Hansoh internally calls H-S-nine-two-six-five. If that architecture sounds familiar, it is because it is essentially the same architecture family as Daiichi's D-X-d platform — humanized antibody, tetrapeptide linker, exatecan-class TOP-one-i payload — which is the platform that gave us trastuzumab deruxtecan on HER two, datopotamab deruxtecan on TROP two, and ifinatamab deruxtecan on B seven H three itself. Same chassis, different antibody. That matters for two reasons — it means the safety profile expectations are already anchored by years of D-X-d-class experience, and it also means that Ris-Rez shares the same cross-resistance liabilities we flagged in the Myricx episode. Everything running through TOP-one-i payload will eventually converge onto the same escape mechanisms.

Now the significance. Small-cell lung cancer has been an A-D-C graveyard for a decade. Rovalpituzumab tesirine — Stemcentrx, DLL-three, pyrrolobenzodiazepine payload — failed spectacularly in TAHOE and MERU and became the category-defining cautionary tale for pursuing SCLC with A-D-Cs. The disease is aggressive, the biology is rough, second-line topotecan sets a survival bar in the six-to-eight-month range, and multiple targeted programs have washed out. What has changed in the last three years is B seven H three coming forward as a broadly and durably expressed target across SCLC and other neuroendocrine tumors, and the D-X-d-class TOP-one-i payload being demonstrably tolerable and active at pivotal doses. Daiichi and Merck's ifinatamab deruxtecan — D-S seven-three-hundred, i-D-X-d — has been the class-leading asset on that thesis, with strong Phase 2 IDEATE-oh-one data and a global Phase 3 in progress. Hansoh has now beaten them to the first Phase 3 O-S readout on the target. That is a big deal for two reasons — first, it validates the B seven H three target class at the pivotal level for the first time, which lifts every asset going after B seven H three regardless of modality. Second, it validates that SCLC can be moved with an A-D-C at all, which reopens a disease space that most people had written off for this modality.

Now, for your world specifically. B seven H three is one of the two or three targets Calibr and adjacent groups are actively building against in T-cell engager, CAR-T, and radioconjugate formats — you saw that in the Novartis-Myricx deal Monday, both of Myricx's lead A-D-Cs are on B seven H three or HER two, and in the ongoing academic B seven H three CAR-T programs in glioblastoma and pediatric solid tumors. A pivotal O-S win on the same target with a totally different modality is the strongest form of target-level de-risking you can get. It tells you the antigen is truly tumor-restricted enough to give a therapeutic window at the level of complete tumor kill. If you are doing a B seven H three T-cell engager or a B seven H three CAR-T, your target hypothesis just got the strongest possible external validation.

Two threads for how to think about this past the headline. Thread one — the ex-China play is G-S-K's global Phase 3, EMBOLD-SCLC-three-oh-one, in relapsed extensive-stage SCLC, with pivotal data expected in twenty-twenty-seven. G-S-K licensed Ris-Rez from Hansoh in late twenty-twenty-three for one-point-seven billion dollars up front and about one-point-five billion in milestones — at the time widely questioned as too expensive for a China-originated asset with a preclinical-heavy dataset. Yesterday's readout retroactively vindicates that deal. Assume G-S-K's revenue model on Ris-Rez in Western markets — SCLC, NSCLC, prostate — anchors somewhere in the two-to-four billion dollar peak range if the effect size holds through the global Phase 3.

Thread two — the Daiichi-Merck response. i-D-X-d is still the class-leading global asset on B seven H three, with a bigger antibody engineering pedigree and Merck's commercial muscle behind it. Daiichi's global Phase 3 IDEATE-oh-one and IDEATE-oh-five in SCLC and NSCLC will read out over the next twelve to eighteen months. Expect Daiichi to accelerate everything they can — filing strategy, indication expansion, combinations with pembrolizumab. What you should watch for is a head-to-head effect-size comparison between Ris-Rez China Phase 3 and i-D-X-d global Phase 3 once both are on the table. Same target, same payload class, different antibody engineering — that is going to be the first clean apples-to-apples read on how much antibody backbone matters within a D-X-d-style A-D-C. The answer to that question will shape a lot of design choices in this class over the next two years.

Bottom line. First Phase 3 O-S win for a B seven H three A-D-C, in a disease that has been an A-D-C graveyard, using a payload class we already know a lot about, from a licensor that G-S-K paid dearly for. Watch the effect-size disclosure at the fall meeting, watch the safety profile in the pooled dataset, and watch for the Daiichi-Merck response — because the second Phase 3 on this target is going to define the class as much as the first one did. That is it for today.
