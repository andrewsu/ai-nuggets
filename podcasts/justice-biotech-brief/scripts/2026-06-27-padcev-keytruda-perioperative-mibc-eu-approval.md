# Padcev + Keytruda — perioperative Nectin-4 ADC + PD-1 wins EU approval in cisplatin-ineligible muscle-invasive bladder cancer; first PD-1 inhibitor plus ADC ever authorized in a curative-intent setting, with a 57-percent pathologic complete response and an EFS hazard ratio of 0.40

## Script

Good morning, Justice. One pick today, and it lands directly on your A-D-C interests but from a regulatory angle that should reset how you think about A-D-C-plus-checkpoint combinations going forward. The European Commission, on Wednesday, June twenty-fourth, approved perioperative pembrolizumab plus enfortumab vedotin — Keytruda plus Padcev — in cisplatin-ineligible patients with resectable muscle-invasive bladder cancer. This is the first time a P-D-1 inhibitor plus an antibody-drug conjugate has been authorized anywhere in the world in a curative-intent perioperative setting, and the supporting phase three data — KEYNOTE-905, also called E-V-303 — are the cleanest evidence the field has of A-D-C and checkpoint synergy in any tumor, in any line.

The setup is what makes the data interpretable. KEYNOTE-905 is a five-hundred-ninety-five-patient three-arm randomized trial in cisplatin-ineligible muscle-invasive bladder cancer — patients whose tumors have invaded the muscle wall but haven't metastasized, who could in principle be cured by radical cystectomy but cannot tolerate the cisplatin-based neoadjuvant chemotherapy that the field has used as standard of care since the nineteen-nineties. About half of muscle-invasive bladder cancer patients land in that cisplatin-ineligible bucket — renal impairment, cardiac comorbidity, performance status — and historically their treatment has been surgery alone with no neoadjuvant systemic component. The three arms of E-V-303 are designed to answer two questions at once. Arm B is the control — surgery alone. Arm A is neoadjuvant Keytruda monotherapy for three cycles, then cystectomy, then fourteen cycles of adjuvant Keytruda. Arm C is the combination — three neoadjuvant cycles of Keytruda plus enfortumab vedotin, then cystectomy, then six adjuvant cycles of the combination plus eight additional cycles of Keytruda monotherapy. Nine enfortumab vedotin doses and seventeen pembrolizumab doses, split across the perioperative window. The primary endpoint is event-free survival, Arm C versus Arm B.

Now the data. The event-free survival hazard ratio for the combination versus surgery alone is zero-point-four-zero, ninety-five-percent confidence interval zero-point-two-eight to zero-point-five-seven, p-value less than zero-point-zero-zero-zero-one — a sixty-percent reduction in the risk of disease progression, recurrence, or death. Median event-free survival in the surgery-alone arm was fifteen-point-seven months. In the combination arm it has not been reached. Overall survival — and this is the load-bearing number — the hazard ratio is zero-point-five-zero, ninety-five-percent confidence interval zero-point-three-three to zero-point-seven-four, p-value zero-point-zero-zero-zero-two. A fifty-percent reduction in mortality. Median overall survival in the surgery arm was forty-one-point-seven months. Median in the combination arm has not been reached. And the pathological complete response rate — the surrogate the field is now genuinely calibrated to in muscle-invasive disease — was fifty-seven-point-one percent in the combination arm versus eight-point-six percent with surgery alone. That fifty-seven-versus-nine spread is the cleanest A-D-C-plus-checkpoint pathological response signal in any solid tumor, perioperative or otherwise.

The intuition is what should land for your work. Enfortumab vedotin is the prototypical A-D-C of the post-Enhertu generation — a Nectin-4-targeting monoclonal coupled to monomethyl auristatin-E, an antimitotic warhead, via a cleavable linker. Nectin-4 is overexpressed in urothelial cancer, the warhead kills the tumor cell when delivered into the lysosome, and the bystander effect from cleaved free auristatin extends the kill radius into nearby antigen-negative tumor cells. What the combination with Keytruda does is engage a second mechanism on top — A-D-C-induced tumor cell death dumps antigen into a tumor-microenvironment that the P-D-1 blockade is simultaneously unmasking. You are running cytotoxic priming and checkpoint release in parallel, in a setting — neoadjuvant, intact primary tumor still in place — where antigen load is at its absolute maximum. The perioperative window is exactly where the immunological synergy should be most pronounced, because the primary tumor mass is the antigen factory and you are preconditioning the immune system before cystectomy removes that source. The fifty-seven-percent pathological complete response is the readout of that priming working at clinical scale.

What this means for the broader A-D-C-plus-checkpoint design space is the bigger take. The Arm A versus Arm C comparison — pembrolizumab monotherapy versus the combination — isn't the headline regulatory number, but the relative effect sizes implied across the three arms is what the next-generation perioperative A-D-C trials are going to be powered against. Every major A-D-C program currently in metastatic disease is looking at this readout as the proof point for moving into earlier-line and curative-intent settings — datopotamab deruxtecan in non-small-cell lung cancer, sacituzumab govitecan in triple-negative breast, the HER3 D-X-d programs, the C-E-A-C-A-M-5 conjugates, the Claudin-eighteen-point-two A-D-Cs. The thesis the field has been waiting to validate is that A-D-Cs work in combination with P-D-1 blockade because the cytotoxic mechanism produces tumor-cell-intrinsic immune visibility — antigen release, calreticulin exposure, type-one interferon induction from the warhead — and that effect should be larger when the immune system has more to work with. Perioperative settings give it more to work with. The Nectin-4-Keytruda data is the first time a P-D-1-A-D-C combination has been formally tested in a curative-intent setting against a control that isolates the surgery baseline, and the answer is unambiguously yes.

The caveats are real and worth keeping in front of you. The combination is not gentle — twenty-seven percent serious adverse reactions in the neoadjuvant phase, one-point-two percent fatal events, fifteen percent permanent discontinuation due to adverse reactions across the studied population of one-hundred-sixty-seven combination-arm patients with assessable toxicity. The most common treatment-related events are the enfortumab vedotin profile — pruritus, alopecia, diarrhea, fatigue, anemia — stacked on top of the Keytruda immune-related adverse-event profile. In a cisplatin-ineligible population that already has comorbidities, that toxicity burden is the trade-off for the response numbers, and the discontinuation rate is the data point to watch as the regimen rolls out into community urology practice. The indication is also explicitly cisplatin-ineligible — there is a parallel E-V-304 trial, KEYNOTE-B15, in the cisplatin-eligible population, and the E-M-A has validated the type-two variation for that indication, with the readout pending and the head-to-head against neoadjuvant cisplatin chemotherapy being the real efficacy bar.

Net take. The E-V-303 readout is now the reference data set for A-D-C-plus-P-D-1 perioperative combinations across solid tumors. For your work on next-generation T-cell-engager combinations and the broader Calibr A-D-C interest, the load-bearing implication is that the combination effect was big enough — pathological-response delta of forty-eight percentage points, overall-survival hazard ratio at half — that A-D-C-plus-checkpoint perioperative combinations stop being a question of whether the synergy is real and start being a question of which tumor antigen, which warhead, and which earlier setting. The Padcev playbook — Nectin-4-targeted A-D-C delivering a microtubule warhead, paired with a P-D-1 monoclonal, neoadjuvant plus adjuvant — is now the template every other A-D-C developer is going to be asked to match against. Worth flagging across the floor.
