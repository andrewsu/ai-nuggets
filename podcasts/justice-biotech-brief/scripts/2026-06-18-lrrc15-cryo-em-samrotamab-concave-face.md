# Justice's Biotech Brief — 2026-06-18

Paper link: https://www.biorxiv.org/content/10.64898/2026.06.16.732218v1

## Script

Good morning, Justice. Today's brief is a structural biology paper out of the Walter and Eliza Hall Institute, posted to bioRxiv overnight, that finally solves the cryo-E-M structure of L-R-R-C fifteen. That's the cancer-associated fibroblast antigen that's been on every A-D-C discovery deck for the last seven years and the target for AbbVie's samrotamab vedotin — A-B-B-V zero-eight-five — which made it into Phase 1 in sarcoma and stalled there. Until this paper, the field has been designing L-R-R-C fifteen binders blind. That just changed.

The setup. L-R-R-C fifteen is a leucine-rich repeat membrane protein, almost absent from healthy adult tissue but heavily upregulated on stromal fibroblasts wrapping desmoplastic tumors — pancreatic, breast, head-and-neck. Expression is induced by T-G-F-beta in the tumor microenvironment, it correlates with therapy resistance, and the textbook play has been: dump cytotoxic payload onto the stroma, kill the fibroblast, collapse the stromal barrier, let immune cells or a co-administered drug get to the tumor. That's the thesis behind samrotamab vedotin. The Phase 1 readout had a tolerable safety profile and a couple of confirmed responses in osteosarcoma and undifferentiated pleomorphic sarcoma, but the program never moved into pivotal trials and AbbVie quietly shelved it. The structural question of how samrotamab actually engaged its target was never answered, and the design space for next-generation L-R-R-C fifteen binders was a black box.

The work. Hydrogen-deuterium exchange mass spec to roughly localize the epitope, then cryo-E-M of the full L-R-R-C fifteen-samrotamab complex at two point six Angstroms — a clean, publication-grade structure of a target nobody had previously crystallized.

Three findings. First, L-R-R-C fifteen is an extended open-arc solenoid — leucine-rich repeats stacked into a curved horseshoe with a concave inner surface and a convex outer surface, both exposed. Second, samrotamab binds laterally on the side of the arc, in the C-terminal repeats closest to the membrane. It does not engage the concave face. That's the punchline. The dominant A-D-C in the clinic is sitting on a surface that may not be the most disruptive site to occupy, which retrospectively rhymes with middling efficacy. Meanwhile the concave face — historically the canonical ligand-binding surface for leucine-rich repeat proteins — is wide open and undrugged. Third, the authors went further and computationally designed de novo minibinders against that concave face. Multiple hits at nanomolar affinity, in-silico-screened and validated in binding assays. They didn't just describe the gap, they handed you a starter kit.

Now the take, for your group. The relevant signal here is not that samrotamab was the wrong molecule. It's that L-R-R-C fifteen has a structurally distinct second surface that nobody has touched. A next-generation L-R-R-C fifteen-directed therapeutic — A-D-C, T-cell engager, multispecific bridging the stroma to a tumor antigen — built on a concave-face binder would have a non-overlapping epitope, no I-P collision with the samrotamab estate, and a plausible mechanistic story for why it might be more potent: occupying a geometrically central surface rather than a peripheral one. The minibinder scaffold is also the right size for a stromal target — small enough to penetrate desmoplastic tissue where full I-g-Gs have diffusion problems, fast clearance for radioligand or imaging versions, and amenable to multispecific formats. A trispecific that bridges a tumor antigen, C-D-three, and concave-face L-R-R-C fifteen would simultaneously activate T cells and tether them to the desmoplastic stroma — a structural argument the field hasn't had access to until now.

Caveats. The structure is the apo binary complex, not L-R-R-C fifteen on the cell surface with T-G-F-beta-induced co-receptors, so any inference about signalling-versus-non-signalling occupancy is provisional. The minibinders are in-vitro nanomolar hits, not validated therapeutics — no cell killing, no pharmacokinetics, no efficacy. And the underlying biology question — does engaging L-R-R-C fifteen on cancer-associated fibroblasts actually translate to durable tumor regression — remains where AbbVie left it. The paper unlocks the chemistry, not the biology.

Net of it. This is a structural-biology paper that resets the playing field for an A-D-C target the field had nearly given up on. Expect the next round of L-R-R-C fifteen-directed programs to be designed against the concave surface, not the lateral one, and to use small-scaffold binders rather than full antibodies. If your group is thinking about stromal targets at all, this is the structure to bookmark.

That's today's brief.
