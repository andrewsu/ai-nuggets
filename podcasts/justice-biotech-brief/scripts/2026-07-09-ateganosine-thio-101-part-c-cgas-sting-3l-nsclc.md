## Script

Good morning, Justice. Today's item is a small-biotech clinical readout with a large mechanistic story. MAIA Biotechnology posted an update yesterday on Part C of their Phase 2 THIO-101 trial. Ateganosine, formerly known as THIO, six-thio-two-prime-deoxyguanosine, sequenced with cemiplimab, delivered a ninety-and-a-half-percent disease control rate — nineteen out of twenty-one — in third-line non-small-cell lung cancer patients who had progressed on both prior checkpoint blockade and prior chemotherapy including docetaxel. The number is eye-catching. The mechanism is the reason to pay attention, and the endpoint choice is the reason to keep the excitement bounded.

Start with the mechanism because it is the interesting part of this program regardless of whether MAIA specifically wins. Ateganosine is a modified nucleotide analog — six-thio-two-prime-deoxyguanosine — that is preferentially recognized and incorporated by telomerase. Telomerase is the enzyme that most cancer cells switch back on to maintain their telomeres and become effectively immortal; healthy adult somatic cells outside a few stem-cell compartments do not express meaningful telomerase activity. So the enzyme that makes cancer cells cancer is also what makes them selectively vulnerable to an analog that only telomerase incorporates. Once inserted into the telomere, the six-thio modification uncaps the chromosome end. Broken telomeric fragments spill into the cytosol as micronuclei, where the DNA sensor cyclic-GMP-AMP synthase — c-GAS — sees them as double-stranded DNA that should not be there. c-GAS activates STING, and STING drives type-one interferon out of the tumor cell and, more importantly, out of infiltrating dendritic cells, which cross-prime tumor-specific CD8 T cells. That primed T-cell response is then what a PD-one inhibitor can unleash. So the sequence — telomerase into c-GAS-STING into cross-primed CD8 into PD-one blockade — is a clean, physiologically anchored path from a cancer-selective DNA-damaging agent to checkpoint re-sensitization in patients whose immune system has already been shown not to respond to PD-one alone. This is the "immunogenic cell death that re-arms an exhausted immune compartment" story the field has been chasing since Zitvogel's early work with anthracyclines fifteen years ago, done with a mechanism that is telomerase-restricted rather than off-target-DNA-damaging.

Sequencing matters here. In THIO-101 the protocol is ateganosine first, then cemiplimab, in twenty-one-day cycles. That is not a hedge; that is the design. You need the telomere damage to happen first so the STING signal is loaded and dendritic cells have already cross-primed a T-cell response by the time PD-one blockade is on board. Simultaneous dosing or reverse-order dosing would break the mechanism.

Now the readout itself. Part C is the expansion cohort of a Phase 2 trial, twenty-one efficacy-evaluable patients as of early July. Every one of them had previously progressed on immunotherapy, on standard chemotherapy, and on docetaxel — this is a truly heavily pretreated, checkpoint-refractory population where standard third-line options run out of gas. Nineteen of twenty-one had disease control by RECIST — no progression at first scan — which is the ninety-point-five percent number. MAIA compares that to twenty-five to thirty-five percent disease control for standard chemotherapy in the same setting. That benchmark is on the low end of what the docetaxel monotherapy literature actually shows, so hold the comparison loosely, but even against a more realistic real-world docetaxel disease-control-rate of forty to fifty percent, a nineteen-of-twenty-one signal in a truly checkpoint-refractory population is not a nothing. Prior Parts A and B in the same trial ran at eighty-eight percent disease control, so this is a consistent within-program signal rather than a one-off.

Where to hold the enthusiasm. First, disease control rate is a soft endpoint — it counts stable disease alongside actual responses, and MAIA has not disclosed the objective response rate breakdown, the depth of response, or duration. Ninety percent disease control from a mix of mostly stable-disease responses is a very different picture than ninety percent from a mix of partial and complete responses. The company will need to show ORR and duration for this to hold. Second, twenty-one patients is small; the statistical uncertainty around a ninety-percent point estimate is wide. Third, MAIA is a nanocap biotech on NYSE American with a small clinical team; the trial is open-label single-arm and the historical benchmark is loose. Fourth, safety is described as "acceptable" without a table — expect some late-emerging on-STING-agonist-related toxicity signals given the innate-immune-activation mechanism.

What actually decides this is THIO-one-oh-four, the pivotal Phase 3 that MAIA started dosing in December of last year. Three-hundred-patient one-to-one randomization against investigator's choice of chemotherapy in the same third-line checkpoint-and-chemo-refractory NSCLC population, overall survival as the primary endpoint. That is the trial that either confirms the ateganosine-cemiplimab sequence as a real thing or does not. Watch for enrollment updates through the second half of this year and for the first interim in twenty twenty-seven.

Read-through for your program space. This is the cleanest current example of a small-molecule immunogenic-cell-death agent designed to work upstream of a PD-one antibody rather than in parallel with it. If it holds, the design lesson generalizes: any cancer-selective DNA-damaging or telomere-damaging modality that reliably fires STING can in principle be positioned as a pre-conditioning arm for TCE, CAR-T, or ADC-driven immune engagement in previously immune-cold or checkpoint-refractory patients. The telomerase-selective delivery vector is the elegant part — every tumor cell has this enzyme running, and no normal tissue does. It is the closest thing to a universal cancer-cell-intrinsic selectivity handle the field has for innate-immune priming.

Bottom line. Ninety-and-a-half percent disease control in a checkpoint-refractory third-line non-small-cell lung cancer cohort of twenty-one patients treated with a telomerase-into-c-GAS-STING-into-PD-one sequence. Encouraging within-program consistency with prior Parts A and B. Objective response rate, duration, safety table, and Phase 3 THIO-one-oh-four confirmatory data are what will determine whether this is a real second act for the c-GAS-STING checkpoint-sensitization thesis or another small-biotech signal that does not survive a randomized trial. Worth tracking. That is it for today.
