## Script

Good morning, Justice. Today's brief is one approval — the F-D-A granted accelerated approval yesterday to Vera Therapeutics for TRUTAKNA, generic name atacicept-vymj, in adult primary I-g-A nephropathy. It is the fifth drug approved for I-g-A-N in about two years, and the first one that hits both B-A-F-F and APRIL together. The mechanism is the interesting part; the label is the story.

Quick reminder on why the mechanism matters. I-g-A nephropathy is driven, in the current textbook picture, by a pathogenic B-cell axis. Circulating plasmablasts and long-lived plasma cells produce galactose-deficient I-g-A-one, which the immune system sees as an autoantigen; autoantibodies form, immune complexes deposit in the glomerular mesangium, and the kidney inflammation and progressive scarring follow. Two cytokines sustain that pathogenic B-cell compartment. B-A-F-F, B-cell activating factor, keeps naive and memory B cells alive and drives their maturation. APRIL, a proliferation-inducing ligand, is more important for the survival and antibody output of the terminally differentiated plasmablasts and plasma cells. Both cytokines converge on a receptor called TACI on the B-cell lineage. Atacicept is exactly that — the extracellular portion of the human TACI receptor fused to an I-g-G one F-c domain, expressed as a soluble decoy. It circulates, binds both B-A-F-F and APRIL, pulls them out of solution, and starves the pathogenic B-cell compartment at the source.

Contrast that with the other approved B-cell axis drug for this disease, Otsuka's Voyxact, generic sibeprenlimab, which was approved late last year. That one is an APRIL-only monoclonal antibody. So the class-defining question in I-g-A-N is now empirical. Does the incremental B-A-F-F neutralization on top of APRIL translate into more clinical benefit, or does it just add cost and infection risk? There is no head-to-head trial. What we have is cross-trial numbers on two similar high-risk-of-progression populations on maximal R-A-S inhibition. TRUTAKNA's ORIGIN 3 interim delivered a forty-two-percent placebo-adjusted proteinuria reduction at thirty-six weeks in two hundred three patients — the F-D-A accepted that as a validated surrogate for kidney progression. Voyxact's VISIONARY delivered a fifty-percent placebo-adjusted proteinuria reduction on the same endpoint. The proteinuria numbers are close. The interesting differentiator on the pharmacodynamic side is the Gd-I-g-A-one biomarker — the pathogenic autoantigen itself. TRUTAKNA drove a sixty-eight-percent Gd-I-g-A-one reduction. That is a signal that dual B-A-F-F-and-APRIL blockade is hitting the plasmablast-plasma-cell compartment harder at the actual biomarker of the disease-driving cell population, and it will be the commercial story Vera's team leads with even though it was an observational secondary endpoint.

Safety was clean enough to matter. In four hundred twenty-eight patients across the full ORIGIN 3 dataset, TRUTAKNA had thirty-two percent infections versus twenty-eight on placebo, so a real but small delta; thirty percent local-injection-site reactions versus five, which is on-mechanism for a weekly subcutaneous auto-injector; no serious opportunistic infections, and importantly no hypogammaglobulinemia. That last point is the one to hold on to. B-A-F-F-plus-APRIL blockade should, in principle, deplete plasma cells over time and drop total I-g-G, and thirty-six-week data not showing hypogamma yet is reassuring but not final — long-term data on this class over years is still forthcoming.

Two threads that matter beyond the label. Thread one — atacicept is a mechanism-repurposing story. Merck-Serono developed this molecule in the 2000s for systemic lupus erythematosus and failed a lupus trial around 2010. Vera picked it up, matched the mechanism to the disease where the pathogenic-B-cell-and-plasma-cell biology aligns cleanly with dual B-A-F-F-APRIL neutralization, ran the pivotal, and shipped it. This is exactly the kind of biology-first indication mapping that A-I-native platform companies are pitching as their forward proposition — a validated regulatory precedent that when you get the mechanism-disease pairing right, a molecule that failed once can succeed the second time.

Thread two — the readthrough for the whole B-cell autoimmune universe. Vertex has povetacicept, which is the second dual-B-A-F-F-and-APRIL fusion protein, from the Alpine Immune acquisition, dosed monthly rather than weekly, and it is running the RUBY-3 phase-three trial in I-g-A-N with readouts likely in the twenty twenty-seven to twenty twenty-eight window. So the head-to-head that matters is atacicept-versus-povetacicept, weekly-versus-monthly, and Vera has a twelve-to-twenty-four-month first-mover window on the dual claim. Both drugs are also in later-stage development in lupus nephritis, systemic lupus, myasthenia gravis, Sjögren's syndrome, and other autoantibody-driven autoimmune indications. The TRUTAKNA approval is the mechanism-class validation that opens all of those secondary indications — for Vera, for Vertex-Alpine, and for anyone building next-generation dual-B-A-F-F-APRIL modalities in your program space.

Bottom line. The first dual-B-A-F-F-and-APRIL inhibitor is approved for I-g-A-N as of yesterday, on a forty-two-percent placebo-adjusted proteinuria reduction plus a sixty-eight-percent Gd-I-g-A-one biomarker suppression, with clean short-term safety and one hypogammaglobulinemia question that a longer follow-up will answer. The disease-specific competitive fight with Otsuka's APRIL-only Voyxact is the near-term commercial event; the mechanism-class fight with Vertex-Alpine's monthly povetacicept is the twenty twenty-seven event. The bigger read is that dual-B-A-F-F-APRIL blockade of the pathogenic B-cell axis is now a regulator-approved mechanism, and every autoimmune indication where plasmablasts and plasma cells are driving autoantibody production is a live indication-expansion candidate. Watch the ORIGIN 3 e-G-F-R confirmatory analysis in the third quarter of this year, TRUTAKNA pricing over the next few weeks, and the Vertex RUBY-3 read in twenty twenty-seven. That is it for today.
