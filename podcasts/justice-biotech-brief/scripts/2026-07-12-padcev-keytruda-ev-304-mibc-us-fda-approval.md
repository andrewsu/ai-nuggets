# Padcev + Keytruda — head-to-head Phase 3 win over neoadjuvant cisplatin chemotherapy in perioperative muscle-invasive bladder cancer (EV-304 / KEYNOTE-B15) delivers the first US FDA approval of a platinum-free perioperative regimen for MIBC regardless of cisplatin eligibility, on 47% event-free-survival and 35% overall-survival risk reductions and a 55.8% versus 32.5% pathologic complete response

## Script

Good morning, Justice. Today's item is the follow-up to the June twenty-seventh episode on the Padcev-plus-Keytruda perioperative bladder cancer story — and it is the harder-efficacy-bar test we flagged at the end of that brief. On Friday July tenth, the F-D-A approved perioperative enfortumab vedotin plus pembrolizumab — Padcev plus Keytruda — as neoadjuvant and adjuvant treatment for muscle-invasive bladder cancer regardless of cisplatin eligibility. This is the first-ever platinum-free perioperative regimen approved for the full muscle-invasive bladder cancer population in the U-S, and the supporting Phase 3 read — E-V-three-oh-four, also called KEYNOTE-B-fifteen — is the study we said in June was the real proof point, because it tested the combination head-to-head against the thirty-year standard of care, neoadjuvant gemcitabine plus cisplatin, in cisplatin-eligible patients. The result — the A-D-C plus checkpoint combination beats cisplatin chemotherapy on every efficacy axis, in a curative-intent setting, against a benchmark the field has been trying to displace since the nineteen-nineties.

Start with the setup, because the trial design is what makes today's read interpretable. E-V-three-oh-four is an eight-hundred-eight-patient global, open-label, randomized Phase 3 in cisplatin-ELIGIBLE muscle-invasive bladder cancer — this is the harder patient population to beat, because the control arm is not surgery alone, it is neoadjuvant gemcitabine-plus-cisplatin followed by cystectomy, which has been the perioperative standard since the mid-nineteen-nineties and is what perioperative regimens have been benchmarked against ever since. Patients were randomized one-to-one. The experimental arm was four cycles of neoadjuvant Padcev plus Keytruda, then cystectomy, then five adjuvant cycles of Padcev plus Keytruda and eight additional cycles of Keytruda monotherapy — total, nine Padcev doses and seventeen Keytruda doses spanning the perioperative window. Control was four cycles of neoadjuvant gemcitabine-cisplatin, then cystectomy, no adjuvant systemic therapy. Primary endpoint, event-free survival. Key secondaries, overall survival and pathologic complete response.

Now the data. Event-free survival hazard ratio for the combination versus cisplatin chemotherapy was zero-point-five-three, ninety-five-percent confidence interval zero-point-four-one to zero-point-seven-oh, p-value less than zero-point-zero-zero-zero-one — a forty-seven percent reduction in the risk of recurrence, progression, or death. Median event-free survival was not reached in the combination arm versus forty-eight-point-five months in the cisplatin arm. Two-year event-free survival was seventy-nine-point-four percent for the combination versus sixty-six-point-two percent for cisplatin chemotherapy. Overall survival — the load-bearing number — hazard ratio zero-point-six-five, confidence interval zero-point-four-eight to zero-point-eight-nine, p-value zero-point-zero-zero-two-nine — a thirty-five percent reduction in mortality. Both O-S medians not yet reached. Two-year O-S, eighty-six-point-nine versus eighty-one-point-three. And the pathologic complete response — the surrogate the field is now genuinely calibrated to in muscle-invasive disease — was fifty-five-point-eight percent in the combination arm versus thirty-two-point-five with cisplatin chemotherapy, a twenty-three-percentage-point absolute delta.

Read those numbers against what we covered in June. The June twenty-seventh episode was E-V-three-oh-three, KEYNOTE-nine-oh-five — the cisplatin-INELIGIBLE population, versus surgery alone. In that setting the effect sizes were larger — hazard ratio zero-point-four-oh on event-free survival, zero-point-five-oh on overall survival, pCR fifty-seven versus eight-point-six — because the control arm was surgery only, no systemic component. E-V-three-oh-four is the cisplatin-ELIGIBLE population, versus the active gold-standard chemotherapy comparator. The effect sizes are smaller in absolute terms — HR zero-point-five-three versus zero-point-four-oh, HR zero-point-six-five versus zero-point-five-oh — but the comparator is much harder. This is the trial that closes the loop. The June approval covered patients who could not tolerate cisplatin. Today's approval, using the two trials read together, covers muscle-invasive bladder cancer regardless of cisplatin eligibility. Cisplatin, as first-line perioperative therapy for M-I-B-C, is essentially displaced.

Now the significance for your work. Three threads. First — the target biology. Padcev is a Nectin-four-targeting monoclonal conjugated to monomethyl auristatin-E through a cleavable linker; MMAE is a microtubule-disrupting warhead with a strong bystander effect. Nectin-four is broadly expressed in urothelial cancer and comparatively spared in normal tissues, which is why the A-D-C hits a therapeutic window at all. What Keytruda adds on top is P-D-1 blockade in the setting where the A-D-C is actively lysing tumor cells and releasing tumor antigens into a checkpoint-permissive microenvironment. The perioperative window — primary tumor still intact, antigen load maximal — is the setting where that mechanistic synergy should be strongest, and today's numbers are the readout of that synergy operating against an aggressive active comparator, not against surgery alone.

Second thread — the design template. Every A-D-C developer running or planning a perioperative program now has E-V-three-oh-four as the reference dataset for what a well-designed A-D-C-plus-P-D-1 combination should look like in curative-intent disease. Not just the numbers — the trial architecture. Nine A-D-C doses across neoadjuvant and adjuvant, seventeen P-D-1 doses spanning the whole perioperative window, tail-adjuvant P-D-1 monotherapy after the A-D-C stops. That is the perioperative playbook. Datopotamab deruxtecan moving into early-stage lung cancer, sacituzumab govitecan into early-stage triple-negative breast, the HER-three D-X-d programs, the Claudin-eighteen-point-two A-D-Cs — all of them will be judged against the shape of the E-V-three-oh-four curve. And for your work specifically on the T-cell-engager and CAR-T side, the design lesson generalizes. Any pharmacology that produces immunogenic tumor cell death in the setting of an intact primary tumor is a candidate for perioperative combination with a checkpoint, and the E-V-three-oh-four numbers are what the field will now expect T-cell-engager-plus-checkpoint perioperative reads to at least approach.

Third thread — the toxicity discount. Grade three or higher treatment-emergent adverse events, seventy-five-point-seven percent on the combination versus sixty-seven-point-two percent on cisplatin chemotherapy. That is a real toxicity burden and it is stacked, because Padcev brings the auristatin profile — skin reactions, peripheral neuropathy, diarrhea — on top of the immune-related adverse-event profile of Keytruda. Grade three-or-higher pneumonitis in the seven-percent range across the population is a serious signal for a curative-intent regimen. The commercial-clinical trade-off is that the perioperative combination is more toxic than cisplatin chemotherapy but delivers a substantially deeper anti-tumor effect and moves patients off platinum. In community urology practice, whether that trade holds depends on adjuvant-phase discontinuation rates and the on-treatment mortality signal in older patients — worth watching in real-world data over the next twelve to eighteen months.

Two things to watch coming out of today. First — the perioperative bladder cancer landscape reshuffles. Standard of care in M-I-B-C is now Padcev plus Keytruda, full stop, and every other perioperative program has to differentiate on top of that. The Merck-Pfizer-Astellas commercial machine turns on. Second — the cross-tumor A-D-C-plus-checkpoint perioperative reads coming in the next twelve to twenty-four months, especially datopotamab-plus-pembrolizumab in early-stage non-small-cell lung. The question they will be asked to answer is whether the E-V-three-oh-four pattern — checkpoint synergy strongest in curative-intent settings with intact primary tumors — generalizes to other tumor types and other A-D-C payload classes.

Bottom line. Padcev-plus-Keytruda just beat cisplatin chemotherapy head-to-head in a Phase 3 perioperative bladder cancer trial, and the U-S F-D-A has extended the label to cover all comers in muscle-invasive disease. First platinum-free perioperative regimen for the full M-I-B-C population, first A-D-C-plus-P-D-1 combination to beat an active platinum comparator in curative intent, and the reference trial for A-D-C perioperative development across solid tumors going forward. That is the brief for today.
