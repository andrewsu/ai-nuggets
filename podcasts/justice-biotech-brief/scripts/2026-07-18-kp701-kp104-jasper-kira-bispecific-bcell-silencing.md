## Script

Good morning, Justice. Today's item is a corporate transaction from two days ago, July sixteenth twenty-twenty-six, that would be easy to file as "another autoimmune reverse-merger" and move on. I want to spend the episode on it instead, because inside the deal is a preclinical bispecific antibody that is a genuinely different design bet in the B-cell-directed autoimmune space — the same design space where you are watching C-D-nineteen C-A-R-T, C-D-twenty monoclonals, and B-cell T-cell engagers compete. The bispecific is called K-P-seven-oh-one, and its mechanism is one you should have a view on.

Here is the short version of the deal, and then I will spend the rest of the episode on the biology and the read-across. Jasper Therapeutics — ticker J-S-P-R, the mast-cell company that had been running the anti-c-kit antibody briquilimab and had been running out of runway after a chronic-urticaria setback — is merging with privately held Kira Pharmaceuticals in an all-stock reverse merger closing this coming Monday, July twentieth. Pre-Jasper shareholders end up with about seven percent of the combined equity. Kira shareholders end up with about half. A one-hundred-and-thirty-two-million-dollar private placement of non-voting convertible preferred stock — led by Affinity Asset Advisors and Ikarian Capital, with Columbia Threadneedle, Balyasny, Silver Arc, Squadron, Nazare, Sirenia, Brahma, and Mirador Therapeutics in the syndicate — funds the combined company through the second half of twenty-twenty-eight. The ticker stays J-S-P-R. Jasper simultaneously divests two Kira legacy assets — K-P-three-oh-one, a long-acting anti-C-five-a monoclonal, and K-P-four-oh-two, a small-molecule C-five-a-receptor antagonist — to Mirador Therapeutics for twelve million dollars upfront plus milestones. Continuing leadership is Jeet Mahal as C-E-O and Herb Cross as C-F-O from Jasper, with Kira's Wenru Song as head of R and D, Greg Keenan as chief medical officer, Matthew Ros as chief operating officer, and Patrick Crutcher as board chair.

Now the two clinical assets that matter, because this is where your beat lives.

The near-term clinical anchor of the combined company is K-P-one-oh-four, brand-name vensobafusp alfa. It is a bifunctional biologic — a single molecule that engages two distinct control points of the complement cascade at the same time. Historically the disclosed construct has been described as a monoclonal antibody against C-five fused to a fragment of factor H. That fusion architecture matters because it addresses a specific failure mode of the current standard-of-care C-five monoclonals, eculizumab and ravulizumab. Blocking C-five downstream stops terminal M-A-C-mediated intravascular hemolysis, but it does not stop upstream C-three-b deposition on P-N-H red cells, which drives residual extravascular hemolysis and leaves a meaningful minority of patients dependent on transfusions. Layering a factor-H fragment onto the same molecule pulls down alternative-pathway amplification upstream — the same lever that a factor-B inhibitor like iptacopan pulls, but delivered as a biologic through a different geometry. In paroxysmal nocturnal hemoglobinuria, the Kira Phase 2 in complement-naive patients reported at A-S-H twenty-twenty-three and updated at A-S-H twenty-twenty-four had all treated patients achieving a two-gram-per-deciliter hemoglobin bump without transfusion and a majority normalizing hemoglobin and L-D-H — sustained through nearly two years of follow-up. The Phase 2 basket program is now running in I-g-A nephropathy and C-three glomerulopathy across China and Australia, with the first stage-one interim readout expected in the fourth quarter of this year and updated data in the second quarter of twenty-twenty-seven, and an end-of-Phase-2 F-D-A meeting after that. In the very-crowded I-g-A-nephropathy field — Fabhalta iptacopan just got its full approval yesterday for that indication, and Vera Therapeutics's Trutakna atacicept came ten days before that — K-P-one-oh-four positions itself as a dual-mechanism biologic against those single-mechanism competitors.

That is the clinical anchor. Now K-P-seven-oh-one, which is the asset in your beat.

K-P-seven-oh-one is a preclinical bispecific monoclonal antibody that co-engages two receptors on the surface of a B cell — C-D-seventy-nine-B, and C-D-thirty-two-B. C-D-seventy-nine-B is the invariant signaling subunit of the B-cell receptor complex — the I-t-a-m-bearing chain that transduces antigen recognition into downstream signaling every time the B-cell-receptor surface immunoglobulin engages its cognate antigen. C-D-thirty-two-B is the F-c-gamma receptor two-B — the only inhibitory member of the F-c-gamma receptor family, bearing an I-t-i-m in its cytoplasmic tail that recruits S-H-P-one and S-H-I-P and shuts down B-cell-receptor signaling when it gets co-clustered with the B-C-R. In normal immunology, that co-clustering is how immune complexes containing I-g-G act as a natural brake on B-cell activation — the antigen bridges the B-C-R while the I-g-G Fc bridges C-D-thirty-two-B, and the resulting proximity drives I-t-i-m phosphorylation and shuts the B cell down. That physiological brake is one of the mechanisms that is broken in autoantibody-driven autoimmune disease, where pathogenic B cells escape peripheral tolerance and keep making self-reactive antibody.

K-P-seven-oh-one is a therapeutic that hijacks that natural brake. By binding both C-D-seventy-nine-B and C-D-thirty-two-B on the same B cell, it enforces the inhibitory-receptor recruitment that a natural immune complex would produce — but without needing an antigen to bridge the B-C-R and without depending on the endogenous antibody-bridging geometry. The design intent is B-cell silencing rather than B-cell depletion. That is the load-bearing distinction, and it is where this asset stops being a follow-on and starts being a modality bet.

Now the read-across for your beat.

The B-cell-directed autoimmune space is currently structured around depletion. C-D-nineteen C-A-R-T — Kyverna, Cabaletta, and the wave of academic centers running I-C-B-taken C-A-R-nineteen products — depletes the peripheral B-cell compartment for months, and the working hypothesis is that reset of that compartment lets the immune system re-tolerize on its own. C-D-twenty monoclonal antibodies — rituximab, obinutuzumab — deplete peripheral C-D-twenty-positive B cells for six-plus months, and their track record in lupus and rheumatoid arthritis is fine but not spectacular. Bispecific T-cell engagers directed at B-cell antigens — the epcoritamabs and mosunetuzumabs of hematology — deplete more thoroughly and are now being repositioned into autoimmune indications by Genmab, Regeneron, Roche and others. The C-A-R-T route offers the deepest depletion but requires apheresis and lymphodepletion. The T-cell-engager route offers off-the-shelf depletion but drags in cytokine release syndrome and neurotoxicity. Anti-B-A-F-F and anti-A-P-R-I-L biologics — belimumab, Vera Trutakna atacicept, telitacicept, ianalumab — attenuate B-cell survival factors rather than depleting the cells themselves, and their profile is more chronic-suppression than immune-reset.

K-P-seven-oh-one is a modality that sits outside all of those. It is not depleting the B cell. It is not blocking a survival cytokine. It is engaging an inhibitory receptor on the B cell's own surface to shut off its B-cell-receptor signaling. Preserved B-cell repertoire, no lymphodepletion, no cytokine release syndrome, no reliance on complement or F-c-effector recruitment to kill the target. If it works, the therapeutic profile is closer to a signaling-modulator like a J-A-K inhibitor than to any of the depleting modalities — but with the specificity of an antibody rather than the systemic distribution of a small molecule.

Whether it will work is the question. Bispecific C-D-thirty-two-B engagers have been an academic target for close to two decades — the concept traces back to work from Ravetch, from MacroGenics, from Xencor — but no bispecific with this specific silencing geometry has cleared clinical development yet, which is why K-P-seven-oh-one is being described as first-in-class. Kira's disclosed timeline is an I-N-D or C-T-A filing in the first quarter of twenty-twenty-seven and first-in-human data in the third quarter of twenty-twenty-seven. The first patients will most likely be in an autoantibody-driven indication where the on-target biology is well-characterized — I-T-P, warm-antibody autoimmune hemolytic anemia, membranous nephropathy, myasthenia gravis, or bullous pemphigoid are the plausible starting points — rather than in a broad indication like lupus. Watch which indication they pick first.

Now what this means for your programs specifically.

For your C-A-R-T work in autoimmune, K-P-seven-oh-one is a competitive modality but a friendly one. It is competing on convenience and tolerability, not on depth of effect. If the C-A-R-T-in-autoimmune thesis is about deep immune reset, K-P-seven-oh-one is about chronic control. Both can coexist. The interesting design question is whether a C-A-R-T-induced reset followed by K-P-seven-oh-one maintenance produces a better long-term outcome than either alone — and whether any of the sponsors are set up to run that combination study.

For your T-cell engager work, the read-across is a design lesson rather than a competitive threat. Bispecific antibodies that engage an inhibitory receptor rather than an activating receptor are a valid and under-explored corner of the modality landscape. Your T-C-E work has been focused on bispecific and trispecific engagers that co-cluster T cells with tumor antigens to activate — the mirror-image problem, of co-clustering an effector cell with an inhibitory receptor to shut it down, is a design space that could apply to the T-regulatory-cell side, to N-K-cell tolerance, and to macrophage polarization work. K-P-seven-oh-one is not the first entry in that space, but it is the first serious bet in an autoimmune-B-cell context, and if the Phase 1 works, it will validate the geometry.

For the J-A-K-A-D-C thesis, K-P-seven-oh-one is another data point in the same direction as bempikibart and A-V-R-oh-oh-one from earlier this week — the oral J-A-K inhibitor class is being pressured by non-J-A-K modalities that address the same clinical problem with cleaner mechanism. K-P-seven-oh-one specifically pressures J-A-K inhibitors in the autoantibody-driven diseases where B-cell control is the therapeutic lever. The J-A-K-A-D-C route defends by restricting where the J-A-K inhibitor distributes; K-P-seven-oh-one attacks by not being a J-A-K inhibitor at all.

Now caveats.

Preclinical is preclinical. No human data, no safety readout, no confirmation that the geometry works in patients rather than just in mice and in in-vitro B-cell activation assays. The first-in-class label cuts both ways — it means differentiation, and it also means an unproven target-modality pair carrying all of the first-in-class risk. The eighteen-month timeline to first-in-human data means the next real inflection point on K-P-seven-oh-one is late twenty-twenty-seven at the earliest. Until then, this asset is a design bet and a story, not a data point.

K-P-one-oh-four in the crowded I-g-A-nephropathy field is the near-term commercial thesis, and it is going to be a hard sell against Fabhalta which just got its full approval yesterday on a hard-eGFR-slope endpoint, Trutakna which just got approved earlier this month on B-A-F-F-A-P-R-I-L blockade, Vanrafia endothelin-A, and the entrenched Filspari. Dual-mechanism differentiation is a real argument, but it needs the fourth-quarter twenty-twenty-six stage-one interim to actually deliver a differentiating I-g-A-nephropathy proteinuria number for the story to hold.

The reverse-merger structure is now the third one you have seen this month — bempikibart's parent Q-thirty-two, A-V-R-oh-oh-one's Avere-NextCure, and now K-P-seven-oh-one's Kira-Jasper. All three used a stalled public shell plus a syndicate P-I-P-E to reach the market pre-Phase-two-b. This is the current template for autoimmune biotech, and the pattern itself is worth understanding — but it also means capital is going to a small number of autoimmune platforms all pursuing overlapping design bets. Consolidation pressure in twenty-twenty-eight will be real.

And the divested C-five-a assets going to Mirador for twelve million dollars up front is a very small number for two clinical-stage complement programs — that tells you the market for anti-C-five-a specifically has cooled hard, probably because avacopan already occupies the C-five-a-receptor lane in A-N-C-A-vasculitis and there is not obvious room for a follow-on unless the differentiation is dramatic.

Bottom line. This is a strategic transaction, not a clinical readout, but the strategic content is unusually rich because it puts two genuinely differentiated modalities into a single company. K-P-one-oh-four is a dual-mechanism complement biologic in a hot renal indication where its Phase 2 interim later this year is a meaningful catalyst against a very crowded field. K-P-seven-oh-one is a preclinical bispecific with the most interesting mechanism-of-action bet I have seen in autoimmune B-cell biology in the last twelve months — silencing pathogenic B cells through their own inhibitory receptor rather than depleting them, with a therapeutic profile that could look more like a signaling modulator than any of the current B-cell-directed depletors. First-in-human data in the second half of twenty-twenty-seven. Watch the indication choice, watch the P-K, and watch whether the geometry produces real B-cell-receptor pathway shutdown in patients. That is the brief.
