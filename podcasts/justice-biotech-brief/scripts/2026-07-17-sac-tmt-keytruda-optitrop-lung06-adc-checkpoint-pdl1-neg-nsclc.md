# Sac-TMT plus KEYTRUDA — first Phase 3 win for an ADC-plus-checkpoint doublet over chemo-plus-checkpoint in first-line PD-L1-negative non-squamous non-small-cell lung cancer (Kelun-Biotech OptiTROP-Lung06 topline, July 14 2026), validating the design bet that a topoisomerase-1 ADC payload can substitute for the platinum backbone in a checkpoint combination

## Script

Good morning, Justice. Today's item is another Merck-adjacent oncology topline from this week, and it lands directly in your A-D-C beat. The Phase three OptiTROP-Lung-oh-six study of sacituzumab tirumotecan — sac-T-M-T, the Trop-two-targeting antibody-drug conjugate that Merck licensed from China's Kelun Biotech in May twenty-twenty-two under the code M-K-two-eight-seventy — read out on Tuesday, July fourteenth. In first-line advanced or metastatic non-squamous non-small-cell lung cancer with P-D-L-one-negative tumors, the sac-T-M-T-plus-Keytruda doublet beat the entrenched standard of care of Keytruda plus pemetrexed plus platinum on progression-free survival at pre-specified interim, with a positive overall survival trend and no new safety signals. Kelun's release calls it the world's first Phase three of an A-D-C combined with an immune checkpoint inhibitor to meet its primary endpoint in the first-line treatment of driver-gene-negative and P-D-L-one-negative non-squamous lung cancer. Scrip framed it more provocatively, as the first chemo-free combination to beat chemo-plus-Keytruda in this front-line setting. That framing is the story.

The trial mechanics first. OptiTROP-Lung-oh-six was randomized, open-label, multicenter, run out of China. Patients had locally advanced or metastatic non-squamous lung cancer, driver-gene-negative, and P-D-L-one tumor proportion score under one percent — the biomarker subset in which chemo-plus-pembrolizumab has been deeply entrenched as front-line standard for the last five years and in which single-agent checkpoint blockade has never had a compelling case. The experimental arm was sac-T-M-T plus pembrolizumab; the control was pembrolizumab plus pemetrexed plus carboplatin or cisplatin, the KEYNOTE-one-eighty-nine regimen. Primary endpoint was progression-free survival by blinded independent central review. Interim hit, positive O-S trend, safety consistent with prior sac-T-M-T studies. No hazard ratio, no confidence interval, no exact median in the release. Kelun is heading to China's Center for Drug Evaluation, and the full data will land at a medical meeting later this year — probably W-C-L-C in September or E-S-M-O in October. Merck holds ex-Greater-China rights from the twenty-twenty-two deal and is running seventeen global Phase threes for M-K-two-eight-seventy across multiple tumor types.

Now the two things that actually matter.

First, the chemo-free framing. The experimental arm has no pemetrexed and no platinum. It replaces the systemic-cytotoxic backbone with Trop-two-targeted delivery of a topoisomerase-one inhibitor — the belotecan-derivative payload K-L-six-one-oh-oh-two-three at a drug-to-antibody ratio of seven-point-four. Whether you call that chemo-free is partly semantic — the A-D-C still delivers a cytotoxin — but from a tolerability, patient-experience, and regulatory-marketing standpoint it absolutely is. No dose-limiting-toxicity platinum backbone with all its neutropenia, thrombocytopenia, nausea, and neuropathy; instead an A-D-C whose off-target burden is stomatitis, cytopenias, and diarrhea from Trop-two-driven distribution to normal epithelia. That is a real quality-of-life difference in a first-line, elderly-skewed population. And in P-D-L-one-negative disease specifically — the biomarker subset that has historically had to accept the roughest regimen because single-agent checkpoint doesn't cut it — dropping the platinum backbone is a major clinical win, not a marketing detail.

Second, and this is the read-across for your beat, this is the first Phase three win for an A-D-C-plus-checkpoint doublet over a chemo-plus-checkpoint doublet in a first-line solid tumor. That has been the design bet of the entire next-generation-A-D-C-in-lung field — datopotamab-deruxtecan plus Keytruda in the T-R-O-P-I-O-N-Lung suite, Enhertu-plus-checkpoint combinations, telisotuzumab-vedotin plus checkpoint — that the payload-driven immunogenic-cell-death signal from a topoisomerase-one A-D-C priming into checkpoint blockade would be strong enough to substitute for the systemic chemotherapy. Until this week the design was thesis-only in the front-line setting. Lung-oh-six converts it into precedent. The mechanistic story you'll now see cited over and over is: topo-one A-D-C payload delivers double-strand-break-mediated tumor-cell death, activates c-G-A-S-STING, drives type-one interferon, feeds dendritic-cell cross-presentation, primes tumor-specific T cells, and pembrolizumab unlocks that primed response. Whether the actual mechanism is that clean is a separate empirical question — but the Phase three data are consistent with it, and every next-generation A-D-C program running a checkpoint combination in front-line solid tumors just got a strong Phase-three-scale reference point to cite.

Now the read-through for your specific programs.

For A-D-C payload chemistry, this validates that the topoisomerase-one inhibitor class — belotecan derivatives here, deruxtecan in the Daiichi-Sankyo payload — is the right payload for checkpoint combinations. Auristatin- and maytansine-payload A-D-Cs will have a harder story to tell in this design space, because their mechanism is more direct microtubule cytotoxicity and less immunogenic-cell-death.

For P-D-one-times-V-E-G-F bispecifics, this raises the follow-on question of whether the bispecific-plus-A-D-C combination — dropping the checkpoint monoclonal in favor of the bispecific and pairing that with the same Trop-two or H-E-R-three A-D-C — could take front-line lung from a doublet to a genuinely differentiated combination. Summit and Akeso's HARMONi-three combines ivonescimab with chemo; the more interesting design in light of Lung-oh-six may be ivonescimab-plus-A-D-C without a chemo partner at all.

For your trispecific T-cell engager work, the shared mechanism-of-action point is instructive rather than direct. Trispecifics deliver T-cell engagement, co-stimulation, and tumor recognition without a cytotoxic payload — but a trispecific combined with an A-D-C in a checkpoint-inflamed subset would be a genuinely three-modality combination worth designing around a topo-one A-D-C partner rather than a chemo partner. That is the design conversation this readout opens.

Now the caveats.

First, no numbers yet. Until we see the hazard ratio, the median P-F-S, the K-M curves and full toxicity tables at the fall meeting, we cannot compare the magnitude of benefit against the reference chemo-plus-Keytruda regimen from KEYNOTE-one-eighty-nine, which sits around a hazard ratio of zero-point-six-five for P-F-S. If sac-T-M-T plus Keytruda pushes that meaningfully lower — say below zero-point-five — you have a genuine global practice change. If it hits an H-R of zero-point-eight, it's a Chinese approval and a footnote.

Second, safety. Sac-T-M-T's real-world tolerability profile includes stomatitis, neutropenia, and cytopenias at rates that in prior monotherapy studies have driven meaningful dose reductions. In combination with pembrolizumab, the specific safety question that the full disclosure needs to address is pneumonitis in a Trop-two-expressing lung — because the immune-related and A-D-C-related pulmonary toxicity signals overlap in the exact tissue you are trying to treat.

Third, geography. This is a Chinese-run trial in a predominantly Chinese population. Merck is running seventeen global Phase threes for M-K-two-eight-seventy including western-population studies. The full sac-T-M-T-plus-Keytruda evidence package for U-S and E-U regulatory purposes is not going to hinge on Lung-oh-six alone.

Bottom line. This is a Chinese-run Phase three from a Chinese biotech whose ex-China rights are held by Merck, and it delivers the first Phase-three win for an A-D-C-plus-checkpoint doublet over a chemo-plus-checkpoint doublet in first-line P-D-L-one-negative non-squamous lung cancer. The chemo-free framing is more marketing than mechanism, but it does signal a design category — payload-driven immunogenic-cell-death from a topo-one A-D-C substituting for the systemic-cytotoxic backbone — that every next-generation A-D-C-plus-checkpoint program has been waiting to see validated at Phase three scale. Numbers to watch when the full readout drops at the fall congress: P-F-S hazard ratio, O-S maturation, and the safety-signal read on pneumonitis and stomatitis specifically. That is the brief.
