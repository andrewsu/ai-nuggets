First, the Insmed T-P-I-P open-label-extension-in-P-A-H story. On Thursday July sixteenth Insmed released twelve-month data from the ongoing open-label extension of its Phase-two-b trial of treprostinil palmitil inhalation powder — the once-daily inhaled dry-powder prodrug of the same treprostinil molecule that United Therapeutics markets as three-times-to-four-times-daily inhaled Tyvaso plus subcutaneous Remodulin plus oral Orenitram — administered at doses up to one-thousand-two-hundred-eighty micrograms once daily in patients with pulmonary arterial hypertension. The efficacy at twelve months. In the T-P-I-P-continued arm of sixty patients who rolled over from active T-P-I-P in the parent Phase-two-b, N-T-pro-B-N-P showed a geometric mean ratio to baseline of zero-point-four-oh — a roughly-sixty-percent reduction in the biomarker that maps most tightly to right-ventricular wall stress in P-A-H. In the placebo-crossed arm of thirty-one patients who switched from placebo to T-P-I-P at twenty-four-weeks, the geometric mean ratio was zero-point-four-one — a nearly-identical roughly-sixty-percent reduction, telling you the biomarker responds to T-P-I-P wherever in the treatment trajectory the drug is introduced.
