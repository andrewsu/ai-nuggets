First, the G-S-K AZUR-one Jemperli rectal-cancer story. Monday July thirteenth G-S-K reported that in the global open-label single-arm Phase 2 AZUR-one trial of dostarlimab as a first-line neoadjuvant monotherapy in stage-two-and-three mismatch-repair-deficient-slash-microsatellite-instability-high locally advanced rectal cancer, the study met its primary endpoint of clinical complete response at twelve months. Trial design. One-hundred-fifty-four patients enrolled, single-arm, nine cycles of five-hundred-milligram dostarlimab intravenously every three weeks over six months, then radiologic and endoscopic and pathologic assessment. In the forty-eight patients who completed the six-month treatment period at data-cut, every one of them achieved clinical complete response and none required surgery to remove the primary tumor. Safety consistent with the established Jemperli profile across solid tumors, no new signals. G-S-K plans regulatory submission and will use the F-D-A Commissioner's National Priority Review Voucher, compressing standard review from ten months to potentially one-to-two months. This is a scaled-up multi-center global expansion of the twenty-twenty-two Andrea Cercek and Luis Diaz Memorial Sloan Kettering paper in the New England Journal of Medicine, where fourteen dMMR-locally-advanced-rectal-cancer patients hit a hundred-percent clinical complete response on dostarlimab monotherapy and none needed surgery, chemo, or radiation. AZUR-one takes that fourteen-patient single-institution signal to a hundred-fifty-four-patient multi-country registrational data set. Jemperli generated eight-hundred-sixty-one-million pounds — about one-point-one-billion dollars — in twenty-twenty-five and is G-S-K's top-selling oncology product; AZUR-one plus the perioperative Phase 3 AZUR-two set up a potential label expansion into the dMMR-slash-MSI-high locally-advanced-rectal-cancer setting, which is roughly five-to-ten percent of the rectal-cancer patient population. Second, the Ipsen Iqirvo ELSPIRE Phase three-b data. Monday July thirteenth Ipsen reported that the ninety-two-patient double-blind placebo-controlled Phase 3-b ELSPIRE trial of Iqirvo — the P-P-A-R-alpha-slash-delta dual agonist elafibranor — hit its primary endpoint of alkaline-phosphatase normalization at week fifty-two with eighty-five percent of Iqirvo-treated patients normalizing A-L-P versus twenty-three percent on placebo, p-value less-than-zero-point-oh-oh-oh-one. The population studied. Adults with primary biliary cholangitis who had inadequate response or intolerance to ursodeoxycholic acid and — this is the important part — with baseline A-L-P levels between one-and-one-point-six-seven times upper limit of normal. That is the milder-A-L-P earlier-stage P-B-C group that had been excluded from the accelerated-approval registrational data set from the ELATIVE trial, which required A-L-P at one-point-six-seven-times-upper-limit-of-normal or higher for enrollment. Ipsen's read is that ELSPIRE potentially doubles the addressable P-B-C population for Iqirvo. Safety consistent with prior known profile; no new signals. Context. Iqirvo was F-D-A-accelerated-approved in June twenty twenty-four as the first-in-class P-P-A-R therapy for P-B-C in combination with U-D-C-A. Its class-competitor Livdelzi seladelpar from Gilead — the selective P-P-A-R-delta agonist Gilead acquired via CymaBay for four-point-three-billion dollars in early twenty-twenty-four — was F-D-A-approved in August twenty-twenty-four. The other former front-runner, Intercept-Alfasigma's Ocaliva obeticholic acid, had its accelerated approval withdrawn in September twenty-twenty-five over post-marketing liver-safety concerns. Read. Iqirvo's earlier-stage-P-B-C-expansion narrative is the P-P-A-R-in-cholestasis franchise-defense against Gilead Livdelzi, and gives Ipsen a genuine long-term commercial edge if the ELSPIRE data hold in real-world use.

Third, the Q-thirty-two Bio bempikibart SIGNAL-A-A Part-B data. Monday July thirteenth Q-thirty-two Bio reported thirty-six-week Part-B topline data from the SIGNAL-A-A Phase 2-a open-label trial of bempikibart in thirty-three patients with severe or very-severe alopecia areata, including subjects who had previously failed J-A-K inhibitors. Bempikibart is a fully-human anti-I-L-seven-receptor-alpha antibody that blocks both I-L-seven and T-S-L-P signaling to re-regulate the aberrant adaptive-immune response driving hair-follicle destruction. Efficacy. Mean thirty-five-point-three-percent reduction in SALT — Severity of Alopecia Tool — score in the modified intent-to-treat population at week thirty-six; forty percent of patients achieved SALT-twenty response and forty-four percent achieved SALT-thirty and SALT-fifty. Q-thirty-two also reported early signs of durable off-drug responses including at least one patient with complete hair regrowth after stopping dosing. Safety mostly mild-and-transient injection-site reactions with no grade-three-plus treatment-related events. Shares closed up nearly sixty-four percent to eighteen-thirty-seven on Monday. Q-thirty-two plans an end-of-Phase-two F-D-A meeting by year-end and Phase 3 registrational-directed initiation in the first half of twenty-twenty-seven. The read for the alopecia-areata mechanism landscape. J-A-K inhibitors — Eli Lilly Olumiant baricitinib, Pfizer Litfulo ritlecitinib, Concert-Sun-Pharma Leqselvi deuruxolitinib — have owned the class but carry black-box warnings; an I-L-seven-receptor-selective antibody hitting patients where J-A-K-inhibitors failed opens a differentiated safety-and-durability positioning play.

And fifth, the Eisai etalanetug A-A-I-C data. Late Sunday July twelfth into Monday July thirteenth Eisai presented at the Alzheimer's Association International Conference in London updated data on etalanetug — code number E-two-eight-fourteen — its investigational anti-microtubule-binding-region-of-tau monoclonal antibody. The result. Etalanetug reduced plasma extended-M-T-B-R-tau-two-forty-three — a tau-tangle-specific pharmacodynamic biomarker — by more than ninety percent at nine months in patients with dominantly-inherited Alzheimer's disease. Cerebrospinal-fluid extended-M-T-B-R-tau-two-forty-three fell sixty-two percent at three months and eighty-nine percent at nine months, with plasma tracking C-S-F closely — seventy-eight percent at three months and greater than ninety percent at nine months. The mechanism. The microtubule-binding region of tau is the domain that seeds tau-tangle propagation from neuron to neuron across the brain, and Eisai's bet is that binding it prevents downstream tau-tangle formation. Etalanetug is in two ongoing trials, the Phase two-and-three Tau NexGen study in dominantly-inherited-A-D combined with Leqembi, and Phase two Study two-oh-two in early sporadic Alzheimer's also on top of Leqembi.
