First, the Celcuity Revtorpyk breast-cancer story. Monday July fourteenth the F-D-A approved Celcuity's Revtorpyk — gedatolisib — as the first-and-only inhibitor of all four class-one P-I-three-K isoforms — alpha, beta, delta, gamma — plus m-T-O-R complexes m-T-O-R-C-one and m-T-O-R-C-two, for treatment of H-R-positive-slash-HER-two-negative locally advanced or metastatic breast cancer without a PIK-three-C-A mutation, following progression on at least one line of endocrine therapy. The approval was based on the pivotal Phase 3 VIKTORIA-one trial. In the PIK-three-C-A-wild-type cohort, Revtorpyk plus palbociclib plus fulvestrant — the triplet — hit median progression-free survival of nine-point-three months versus two-point-oh months for fulvestrant alone, a hazard ratio of zero-point-two-four, meaning a seventy-six-percent reduction in risk of progression or death. Objective response rate thirty-two percent versus one percent on fulvestrant. Median duration of response seventeen-point-five months. The doublet — Revtorpyk plus fulvestrant without palbociclib — hit seven-point-four-month progression-free survival, hazard ratio zero-point-three-three, sixty-seven percent risk reduction, twenty-eight-percent objective response rate. Safety includes serious stomatitis at seventy-two percent all-grade with the triplet, twenty-two percent grade three, plus rash and hyperglycemia — F-D-A label mandates prophylactic mouthwash to manage stomatitis. Shares closed at a hundred-eleven dollars Monday. Commercial launch expected late Q-three twenty twenty-six. Celcuity plans an s-N-D-A filing in Q-three twenty twenty-six for the PIK-three-C-A-mutant cohort as well — the mutant cohort separately doubled progression-free survival versus alpelisib-plus-fulvestrant standard of care. Second, the Biogen-Ionis diranersen A-A-I-C tau-antisense story. Tuesday July fourteenth Biogen presented Phase 2 CELIA data on diranersen — B-I-I-B-oh-eight-oh — a tau-directed antisense oligonucleotide that binds tau's precursor messenger-R-N-A and gums up the genetic instructions the neuron uses to make new tau protein. Four hundred sixteen patients with mild cognitive impairment or mild Alzheimer's disease, three dose arms of intrathecal diranersen every six months over eighteen months, versus placebo. The efficacy result. Diranersen produced fifty-to-sixty-five-percent cerebrospinal-fluid total-tau reductions plus robust brain tau P-E-T reductions across all three doses, which Biogen called unprecedented target-engagement magnitude. On the cognitive front, the lowest sixty-milligram dose — the surprise result — showed a twenty-six-percent slowing of decline on the Clinical-Dementia-Rating-Sum-of-Boxes scale, comparable to Leqembi's twenty-seven percent in early A-D. Multiple secondary cognitive endpoints slowed by twenty-three-to-fifty percent. The catch. The trial's primary endpoint was a monotonic dose-response — bigger doses give bigger effects — and the data went the other way. Higher doses were worse than the low dose on the cognitive endpoints. This is unusual for an antisense drug and Biogen and Ionis will need to explain it before Phase 3. Biogen is proceeding to Phase 3 anyway, prioritizing the sixty-milligram dose. The commercial and pipeline read. Diranersen is one of the first tau-directed A-D therapies with a plausible cognitive-benefit signal — B-Riley Securities called it a de-risking event for the tau class. Cantor Fitzgerald is more cautious, noting a lower probability of Phase-3 success given the inverse dose response. Biogen owes Ionis up to five-hundred-eighty million dollars in Phase-3-milestone payments to advance. The competitive read. This lands alongside Eisai's etalanetug — the anti-microtubule-binding-region-of-tau monoclonal antibody — which produced greater-than-ninety-percent plasma tau-two-forty-three biomarker reductions at A-A-I-C this past Sunday-into-Monday. Amyloid-plus-tau combination therapy is the emerging two-mechanism A-D therapeutic bet, and Biogen-Ionis and Eisai each now have a tau asset in that combination race.

Third, the AstraZeneca-Dizal Zegfrovy licensing deal. Tuesday July fourteenth AstraZeneca announced a global exclusive license agreement for Zegfrovy — sunvozertinib — the oral irreversible E-G-F-R inhibitor developed by Dizal, an AstraZeneca-Chinese-spinoff biotech based in Shanghai. Zegfrovy is approved in the U-S and China in E-G-F-R-exon-twenty-insertion locally advanced or metastatic non-small-cell lung cancer post-platinum chemo — a niche molecular subset representing roughly two-to-three percent of E-G-F-R-mutant lung cancer. Deal terms. Six-hundred-million dollars upfront plus up to four-hundred million in development milestones plus five-hundred million in sales milestones plus tiered royalties — one-point-five-billion dollars total plus royalty tail. The strategic rationale. Dizal recently reported positive global Phase 3 WU-KONG-twenty-eight data in first-line E-G-F-R-exon-twenty-insertion non-small-cell lung cancer, a thirty-five-percent reduction in progression or death versus platinum chemotherapy, published in the New England Journal of Medicine at A-S-C-O in June. That reads across to a much larger addressable population if the first-line label goes through. AstraZeneca inherits an approved-and-launched E-G-F-R asset with expansion runway, complementing its Tagrisso osimertinib franchise. Dizal keeps mainland-China rights and the Chinese commercial footprint. This is another Chinese-license-flowing-westward deal on top of the wave of hundred-plus deals since early twenty twenty-five — the pattern is fully entrenched.

Fourth, the HUYABIO tucidinostat plus nivolumab melanoma Phase 3. Tuesday July fourteenth privately-held HUYABIO International reported statistically significant Phase 3 topline in advanced melanoma with H-B-I-eight-thousand — tucidinostat, chidamide, also branded Epidaza and Hiyasta in some markets — an oral class-one selective H-D-A-C inhibitor targeting H-D-A-C-one, H-D-A-C-two, H-D-A-C-three, and H-D-A-C-ten. Trial design. Randomized, global, four-hundred-four patients across fifteen countries, H-D-A-C inhibitor plus nivolumab versus nivolumab plus placebo in advanced melanoma. Primary endpoint. Progression-free survival — eleven-point-seven months on the combination versus seven-point-four months on nivolumab-plus-placebo, a fifty-eight-percent relative improvement. The mechanism intuition. H-D-A-C inhibition remodels tumor-cell chromatin and, combined with checkpoint inhibition, produces the anti-tumor immune synergy that Merck-Syndax entinostat plus pembrolizumab could not deliver in the E-C-H-O-three-oh-two Phase 3 melanoma readout in twenty-nineteen. That prior failure hurt the H-D-A-C-plus-checkpoint thesis broadly. HUYABIO is a Chinese-U-S drug-development company; if the full data hold up on the O-S secondary endpoint, this is the first Phase 3 win for the H-D-A-C-plus-checkpoint mechanism in solid tumors and revives the class.
