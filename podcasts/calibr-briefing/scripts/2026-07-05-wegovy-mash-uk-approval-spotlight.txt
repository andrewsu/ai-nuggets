Spotlight, Sunday July fifth. Friday the UK Medicines and Healthcare products Regulatory Agency granted conditional marketing authorization to Novo Nordisk's Wegovy — semaglutide, two-point-four milligrams once weekly, with a permitted escalation to seven-point-two milligrams weekly in obese patients — for adults with non-cirrhotic metabolic-associated steatohepatitis and moderate-to-advanced liver fibrosis. This is the first GLP-one to carry a MASH label outside the U-S. F-D-A accelerated-approved semaglutide for the same indication in August twenty twenty-five. The U-K approval is conditional — the National Institute for Health and Care Excellence has to complete a cost-effectiveness appraisal before the NHS reimburses — but the label is now on the pack. Six threads.

First, the disease and why MASH is a large under-treated category. Metabolic-associated steatohepatitis — the condition formerly known as N-A-S-H, renamed in twenty twenty-three to align with metabolic-associated fatty liver disease nomenclature — affects an estimated one-hundred-fifteen million adults globally and roughly twenty-two million in the U-S. Prevalence tracks with the obesity and type-two diabetes epidemic — about thirty percent of obese adults and up to seventy-five percent of patients with type-two diabetes have some degree of metabolic-associated fatty liver disease, and about a third of those progress to MASH with hepatocyte injury and inflammation. Untreated MASH progresses to fibrosis, cirrhosis, hepatocellular carcinoma, and end-stage liver disease. MASH is projected to overtake hepatitis C as the leading cause of liver transplantation in the U-S within the decade. The historical treatment stack has been diet, exercise, and metabolic-comorbidity management — nothing directly disease-modifying was approved until Madrigal's Rezdiffra in March twenty twenty-four. That is a very short window to get from zero approved therapies to the two-drug class with two mechanisms that MASH now has as of the U-K approval.

Second, the ESSENCE Phase three data that produced the label. ESSENCE is Novo Nordisk's Phase three trial of semaglutide two-point-four milligrams weekly in adults with MASH and moderate-to-advanced liver fibrosis — F-two or F-three histology. Two-part design. Part one — biopsy-confirmed histologic endpoints at seventy-two weeks in the first eight-hundred randomized patients. Part two — liver-related clinical events over two-hundred-forty weeks in the full one-thousand-one-hundred-ninety-seven-patient enrollment, ongoing. Two-to-one randomization semaglutide to placebo. Both primary endpoints at seventy-two weeks hit with wide margins. First primary — resolution of steatohepatitis without worsening of fibrosis — sixty-two-point-nine percent semaglutide versus thirty-four-point-three percent placebo, estimated difference in responder proportions twenty-eight-point-seven percent, ninety-five-percent confidence interval twenty-one-point-one to thirty-six-point-two, p less than zero-point-zero-zero-one. Second primary — fibrosis improvement without worsening of steatohepatitis — thirty-seven percent versus twenty-two-point-five percent, again highly statistically significant. Weight loss at seventy-two weeks — ten-point-five percent from baseline on semaglutide versus two percent on placebo. Part two — the two-hundred-forty-week liver-clinical-events primary — is where the hazard-ratio-on-cirrhosis-and-decompensation number will land. That readout is late twenty twenty-seven at the earliest.

Third, the mechanism read and why GLP-one works in MASH. Semaglutide is a GLP-one receptor agonist — the same class as Ozempic and Wegovy for diabetes and obesity — and its MASH activity was for years assumed to be entirely weight-loss-mediated. Weight loss of ten-plus percent is itself a highly effective MASH intervention: sustained ten-percent weight loss produces MASH resolution in roughly ninety percent of patients in observational cohorts. But post-hoc ESSENCE analyses that Novo presented at E-A-S-L in June twenty twenty-six show liver-histology benefits that are not fully explained by weight loss — subgroup analyses in patients with less than five percent weight loss still show significantly higher MASH-resolution rates on semaglutide than placebo. That suggests direct GLP-one signaling on hepatic lipid handling and inflammatory pathways in addition to the weight-loss-mediated benefit. Mechanistically this makes sense — GLP-one receptors are expressed on hepatic Kupffer cells and there is emerging evidence for direct anti-inflammatory hepatic effects. For the class biology, this pushes MASH out of a pure "obesity-comorbidity" frame and into a mechanism-plus-comorbidity frame.

Fourth, the competitive landscape and Madrigal Rezdiffra as the reference case. Madrigal's Rezdiffra — resmetirom, an oral thyroid hormone receptor-beta agonist — got F-D-A accelerated approval in March twenty twenty-four as the first MASH-native compound. Rezdiffra's mechanism is direct hepatocyte-signaling: T-H-R-beta activation reduces hepatic lipid content, improves mitochondrial function, and lowers liver fat by roughly thirty percent. Rezdiffra's launch has been the biggest first-year specialty launch in modern hepatology — nine-hundred-fifty-eight-point-four million dollars in twenty twenty-five sales, three-hundred-eleven-point-three million in first-quarter twenty twenty-six representing one-hundred-twenty-seven percent year-over-year growth, on a trajectory to blockbuster status by the end of twenty twenty-six and consensus roughly four-point-eight billion dollars in twenty thirty-one. Madrigal also has E-U approval as of June twenty twenty-six. The competitive question after Wegovy's U-S MASH approval in August twenty twenty-five was whether Rezdiffra sales would erode. The one-quarter-post-Wegovy-approval read was — no. Madrigal C-E-O Bill Sibold said Wegovy's approval was "certainly not to the detriment of Rezdiffra" and pointed to the highest new-to-brand-prescription week since launch. The market appears to be splitting: obese MASH patients get GLP-one, non-obese or GLP-one-intolerant patients get Rezdiffra, and a growing subset combines the two. That is a very different market from the winner-take-all frame that pre-launch consensus assumed.

Fifth, the wider MASH pipeline and how the U-K label repositions the class. The MASH-therapeutic pipeline is one of the busiest in hepatology. Behind Rezdiffra and semaglutide the next tier includes — Akero Therapeutics' efruxifermin, an F-G-F-twenty-one analog with Phase three read-out expected late twenty twenty-six that has shown deep histologic responses at twenty-four weeks in Phase two-b — 89bio's pegozafermin, another F-G-F-twenty-one, similar timing — Boehringer Ingelheim's survodutide, a GLP-one-glucagon dual agonist with Phase three underway — and Eli Lilly's tirzepatide (Mounjaro/Zepbound) which is expected to file for MASH on the SYNERGY-NASH Phase two-b data showing fibrosis-improvement rates competitive with semaglutide, plus early data on retatrutide, Lilly's tri-agonist. The U-K conditional approval for Wegovy is a positive lift for the entire GLP-one-plus-incretin class — regulators have now endorsed the metabolic-mechanism-upstream route as an approvable path for MASH, which lowers the regulatory-approvability risk for tirzepatide and survodutide filings. For F-G-F-twenty-one, the read is that the market splits by mechanism — GLP-one-plus-incretin for obese MASH, T-H-R-beta or F-G-F-twenty-one for lean MASH and combination therapy. For Foresite, the F-G-F-twenty-one and T-H-R-beta classes both intersect with portfolio interests and the U-K signal is favorable across the field.

Sixth, the U-K approval as a payer story and the E-U path. The U-K conditional label is a marketing authorization — Wegovy can be prescribed and dispensed for MASH — but the NHS reimbursement pathway runs through NICE cost-effectiveness appraisal. NICE evaluates quality-adjusted-life-year cost benchmarks that MASH-therapeutic drugs have been struggling to clear historically because MASH is asymptomatic until decompensation and Q-A-L-Y gains from disease-modifying therapy show up only over long horizons. Rezdiffra's NICE appraisal is still pending. Wegovy in obesity got a partial NHS pathway through direct-primary-care access rather than specialist-only, which improved patient access. The MASH pathway will likely require MASH-specialist referral. E-M-A conditional or full approval is the next regulatory catalyst — Novo has filed and the E-M-A Committee for Medicinal Products for Human Use is reviewing. The U-K approval historically leads the E-M-A on Wegovy by a few months. For the field, the U-K signal probably compresses the E-M-A timeline.

Bottom line. UK MHRA granted conditional marketing authorization Friday to Novo Nordisk's Wegovy — semaglutide two-point-four milligrams weekly, escalating to seven-point-two milligrams in obese patients — for adults with non-cirrhotic MASH and moderate-to-advanced liver fibrosis. First GLP-one MASH label outside the U-S, based on ESSENCE Phase three showing sixty-three-percent MASH resolution and thirty-seven-percent fibrosis improvement at seventy-two weeks versus placebo, both endpoints highly statistically significant. For Novo Nordisk, this is a third megabase indication on top of obesity and cardiovascular risk reduction and the Wegovy franchise gets a MASH-specific label that positions the drug for a decade-plus in hepatology. For Madrigal, the one-quarter-post-U-S-approval read shows Rezdiffra sales holding — Wegovy expands the addressable market rather than cannibalizing it, and Madrigal's E-U approval in June positions Rezdiffra symmetrically to Wegovy on both sides of the Atlantic. For the wider MASH pipeline — Akero and 89bio's F-G-F-twenty-one, Boehringer's survodutide, Lilly's tirzepatide and retatrutide — the U-K regulatory endorsement of the GLP-one metabolic-mechanism route is a positive lift for approvability of the whole incretin class in MASH and probably compresses timelines for tirzepatide and survodutide filings. For Foresite and Calibr, the read is that MASH is now a two-mechanism-class category with a growing pipeline and evolving combination logic — GLP-one-plus-hepatocyte-targeted-therapy is the emerging combination that will define the next five years of the market, and any Foresite portfolio company positioned on a hepatocyte-native axis (F-G-F-twenty-one, T-H-R-beta, P-P-A-R) needs a clear thesis on combination positioning against a GLP-one-backbone standard-of-care. Next catalysts. One, E-M-A Committee for Medicinal Products for Human Use opinion on Wegovy MASH — likely third-quarter twenty twenty-six. Two, ESSENCE Part two two-hundred-forty-week liver-clinical-events readout — twenty twenty-seven or later. Three, Akero efruxifermin Phase three readout — late twenty twenty-six. Four, Lilly tirzepatide MASH filing — twenty twenty-six or early twenty twenty-seven. Five, NICE cost-effectiveness verdicts on both Rezdiffra and Wegovy in MASH — twelve to eighteen months.
