First, the Novartis Fabhalta full-approval-in-primary-I-g-A-N story. Second, the GSK camlipixant Phase-three failure story. On Friday July seventeenth GSK announced discontinuation of camlipixant in refractory chronic cough after the CALM-one and CALM-two Phase-three pivotals delivered inconsistent results — CALM-one met the twenty-four-hour cough-frequency primary endpoint at fifty-milligrams-twice-daily at the twelve-week timepoint, but CALM-two missed on the same dose-and-endpoint at the twenty-four-week timepoint, the twenty-five-milligrams-twice-daily arm missed both trials, and key secondary Chronic-Cough-Diary endpoints missed across the board. Where camlipixant goes now.
