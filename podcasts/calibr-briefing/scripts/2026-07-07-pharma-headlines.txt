First, Vertex buys Crinetics for ten billion. Yesterday afternoon Vertex announced agreement to acquire Crinetics Pharmaceuticals for approximately ten billion dollars in cash — eighty-five dollars per share, roughly a one-hundred-one percent premium to Friday's closing price, four-and-a-half billion dollars of bridge financing committed by Bank of America and Morgan Stanley on top of Vertex's balance-sheet cash. Crinetics is a Neurocrine-Biosciences-alumni-founded San Diego biotech founded in twenty-oh-eight by C-E-O Scott Struthers and colleagues, focused on nonpeptide small-molecule G-protein-coupled-receptor drug discovery in endocrinology. Vertex is buying two assets. Second, BMS Krazati confirmatory fails in colorectal cancer. Presented over the weekend at ESMO Gastrointestinal Cancers Congress in Barcelona, the KRYSTAL ten Phase 3 confirmatory of adagrasib plus cetuximab against modern chemotherapy in second-line K-RAS G-twelve-C-mutant metastatic colorectal cancer missed both primary endpoints. Four hundred sixty-one patients randomized. Progression-free survival — seven-point-five months on Krazati-plus-cetuximab versus eight-point-one months on chemotherapy — hazard ratio zero-point-eight-nine, ninety-five-percent confidence interval zero-point-seven-one to one-point-one-three, p-value zero-point-three-two. Overall survival at minimum twenty-six-point-four months follow-up — twenty-one-point-six months versus twenty-one-point-seven months — hazard ratio zero-point-eight-three, ninety-five-percent confidence interval zero-point-six-seven to one-point-oh-three, p-value zero-point-oh-nine. Objective response rate was strongly in Krazati's favor — forty-seven percent versus sixteen percent, complete responses seven percent versus under one percent — but that response advantage did not translate into progression-free-or-overall-survival benefit. Roughly thirty percent of chemo-arm patients crossed over to a subsequent K-RAS G-twelve-C inhibitor, which likely diluted the O-S signal. B-M-S will discuss next steps with F-D-A; the twenty twenty-four accelerated approval based on KRYSTAL one is now formally at risk. Contrast — Amgen's sotorasib-plus-panitumumab combo hit its primary P-F-S endpoint in CodeBreaK 300 last year and F-D-A converted that program to full approval in January twenty twenty-five. Different E-G-F-R partner, different comparator arm, different line-of-therapy population, different outcome. The class isn't dead in colorectal — B-M-S's specific regimen and design lost. For Revolution Medicines the read is that broader R-A-S coverage — pan-K-R-A-S G-twelve-D via daraxonrasib and zoldonrasib — is precisely the differentiation argument that gets stronger every time a single-K-RAS-mutant-plus-E-G-F-R doublet trips in C-R-C.

Third, Vera atacicept P-D-U-F-A today. F-D-A action on Vera Therapeutics's atacicept — a recombinant B-A-F-F-and-APRIL dual-neutralizing fusion protein for I-g-A nephropathy — was expected on or around today. As of this recording no F-D-A action wire has hit. The BLA is under priority review, breakthrough therapy designation, and the accelerated approval pathway. Pivotal ORIGIN 3 Phase 3 data — published in the New England Journal in November twenty twenty-five — showed a forty-six-percent proteinuria reduction from baseline at thirty-six weeks, forty-two percent placebo-adjusted p less than zero-point-oh-oh-oh-one, sixty-eight-percent reduction in galactose-deficient-I-g-A-one, hematuria resolution in eighty-one percent of patients. Delivery is once-weekly subcutaneous auto-injector at home. Full approval hinges on the revised e-G-F-R analysis pulled forward to the third quarter of twenty twenty-six and an s-B-L-A in the fourth quarter, with full approval possible twenty twenty-seven. Competitive landscape. Otsuka's Voyxact — sibeprenlimab, APRIL-only monoclonal — got accelerated approval in November twenty twenty-five. Novartis's Fabhalta — iptacopan, factor-B complement inhibitor — is competing on a separate mechanism. Calliditas Tarpeyo delayed-release budesonide and Travere Filspari — sparsentan, dual endothelin-plus-angiotensin-receptor blocker — round out the market. And notably, Vertex holds povetacicept — the other B-A-F-F-slash-APRIL dual mechanism, acquired via the twenty twenty-four Alpine Immune Sciences deal — in Phase 3 development for the same indication. Vertex is now buying Crinetics at ten billion dollars the same week its I-g-A-N asset is competing directly against Vera's imminent approval decision. Approval today would open a four-mechanism competitive market for I-g-A-N and set up an atacicept-versus-povetacicept dual-B-A-F-F-APRIL head-to-head over the next twelve to eighteen months.

Fourth, Genentech pruning plus Astex four hundred ninety million. Yesterday Genentech confirmed one hundred three role eliminations across the g-RED research organization effective July twenty-ninth, closing the infectious disease and physiological chemistry units and downsizing early clinical development, development sciences, and translational medicine. Three long-tenure V-P departures — Vishva Dixit, V-P and senior fellow in physiological chemistry, twenty-nine years, apoptosis and cell-death-pathway pioneer; Man-Wah Tan, V-P and senior fellow in infectious diseases, sixteen years; Todd McDevitt, V-P and head of cell therapy, three years. Adds to at least four hundred eighty-nine layoffs across g-RED in twenty twenty-five for a running total near five-ninety-two across the last eighteen months. Simultaneously Genentech signed a research collaboration with Astex Pharmaceuticals — the Otsuka subsidiary — for a preclinical breast cancer program targeting a cell-cycle-dependent regulator developed via Astex's fragment-based-drug-discovery platform. Twenty-five million dollars up front, up to four hundred ninety million dollars in milestones plus tiered royalties. Two reads. One, the "cut internal, buy external" pattern at Genentech is now structural — the pharma with the historically strongest internal discovery brand is systematically converting internal capacity to external biobucks-collaboration spending on a roughly twenty-to-one basis when you compare up-front outlay to head-count cut. That is a talent-availability signal for Bay Area early-discovery hiring — Dixit and Tan are two of the most senior scientific voices in g-RED and both are now in the market. Two, the specific decision to close the physiological chemistry unit is the sharper strategic call — Dixit built the apoptosis and cell-death-signaling franchise that produced venetoclax's target biology among a long list of contributions. Closing that unit while paying Astex four hundred ninety million for a cell-cycle-dependent target reads like a bet that fragment-based-drug-discovery-plus-Astex crystallography beats internal cell-biology-plus-medicinal-chemistry for the next generation of small-molecule oncology.

Fifth, Scribe Therapeutics files S-one. Berkeley-based Scribe Therapeutics filed its S-one over the weekend for a Nasdaq I-P-O — placeholder seventy-five million dollars — on an epigenetic-silencing platform called E-L-X-R built on machine-learning-engineered CasX enzymes. Lead asset is S-T-X eleven fifty, an L-N-P-delivered CRISPR epigenetic silencer targeting P-C-S-K-nine for durable L-D-L cholesterol lowering without D-N-A editing. First-in-human Phase 1 in Australia enrolled up to sixty-four patients, initial data expected first-half twenty twenty-seven. Twenty-five million dollars C-I-R-M award for cardiometabolic programs; prior partnerships with Biogen, Sanofi, and Lilly-Prevail. Backed by Andreessen Horowitz, Avoro, and OrbiMed at roughly one hundred twenty million dollars raised pre-I-P-O. Two reads. One, this is the first I-P-O test for epigenetic-silencing as a modality distinct from D-N-A base-editing — a direct competitive shot at Verve Therapeutics's V-E-R-V-eight-oh-two P-C-S-K-nine base editor, Amgen's Repatha monoclonal, and Novartis's Leqvio si-R-N-A. The differentiation argument is that transient chromatin-level silencing without D-N-A cutting sidesteps the off-target-editing and permanence concerns that dogged the first wave of in-vivo C-R-I-S-P-R programs. Two, this fits the A-I-in-drug-discovery bucket cleanly — Scribe's CasX enzymes were computationally engineered from bacterial Cas variants using directed-evolution-plus-machine-learning workflows. The I-P-O pricing will be a real-money test of whether public markets will pay for machine-learning-plus-enzyme-engineering platform stories at preclinical-to-Phase-one stage, which is exactly the valuation question that flows through to Xaira, Iambic, Isomorphic Labs, and the rest of the A-I-native discovery peer set.
