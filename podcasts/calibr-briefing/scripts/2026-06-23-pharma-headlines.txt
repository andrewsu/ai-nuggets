Good morning, Travis. Tuesday June twenty-third, twenty twenty-six. Day two of BIO in San Diego. The AbbVie-Apogee rumor we previewed yesterday is now signed, and a CAR-T solid-tumor approval landed out of China overnight that's arguably the more important story of the week. Six items.

First — and I'll spend the spotlight on it — AbbVie agreed yesterday afternoon to acquire Apogee Therapeutics for about ten point nine billion dollars in cash, one hundred thirty-five dollars and eleven cents a share, a forty-nine percent premium to last Friday's close. Lead asset is zumilokibart, also called A-P-G-seven-seven-seven, a half-life-extended anti-interleukin-thirteen monoclonal aimed at quarterly to twice-yearly dosing for atopic dermatitis and asthma. Apogee was a Fairmount and Venrock spinout from Paragon Therapeutics in twenty twenty-two, IPO'd in twenty twenty-three. RBC called the price ideal; Stifel sees mega-blockbuster potential. This is AbbVie's biggest bolt-on since Allergan in twenty nineteen, the clearest tell that the post-Humira immunology franchise is being built on ultra-long-acting biologics, and it goes deep in a few hours.

Second, CARsgen Therapeutics won NMPA approval out of China for satricabtagene autoleucel — satri-cel — the world's first CAR-T cell therapy approved in a solid tumor. The target is Claudin-eighteen point two, the indication is Claudin-eighteen-point-two-positive, HER2-negative advanced gastric and GEJ adenocarcinoma after at least two prior lines. Pivotal Phase 2 numbers: fifty-five percent objective response rate, ninety-six percent disease control rate. Solid tumors have been the structural failure mode of CAR-T for fifteen years, and the field has been waiting for the first approval to break. CARsgen got there with a CLDN-eighteen-point-two construct — the same target Astellas built zolbetuximab against in the same patient population — and the read-through is that selective, gut-restricted antigens are the right entry point for solid-tumor CAR-T, not the bulk-expressed antigens that drove all the early attempts. Worth tracking which Western CAR-T platforms now redirect toward this template; the BIO floor will be loud on this all week.

Third, Definium Therapeutics posted Phase 3 results in major depression for DT-one-twenty, an orally disintegrating LSD tablet. Single hundred-microgram dose, a thirteen-point-three-point drop on MADRS at week six versus five-point-two on placebo — eight-point-one points placebo-adjusted, met the primary and all key secondaries, no new suicidality signal. The CEO called it the best Phase 3 depression data he's ever seen, and the magnitude on a single-dose mechanism with durability out to six weeks is genuinely category-bending. This is the first Phase 3 for LSD in MDD, and the entire psychedelic class has lived under the shadow of two failed COMP-three-six-zero ketamine-comparator pivotals over the last two years — Definium just put the class back on the FDA table.

Fourth, an anti-TL1A double-tap that connects back to our Spyre spotlight from a week ago Tuesday. Merck's tulisokibart — the Prometheus-acquired anti-TL1A monoclonal, formerly P-R-A-zero-two-three — hit primary and all key secondaries in ATLAS-UC, the first Phase 3 win for any TL1A biologic in ulcerative colitis. Same morning, Bionyra Pharma launched on a one-hundred-sixty-five-million Series A co-led by Jeito and Sofinnova, with two TL1A assets in-licensed from TrueLab — an extended half-life anti-TL1A and a TL1A-by-IL-twenty-three-p-nineteen bispecific. Spyre's bet from last week is now visibly the consensus pharma read on the cytokine: Merck has the validated single-target Phase 3 win, Spyre has the long-acting subQ version, Bionyra is the bispecific. The IBD biologics shelf is reorganizing around TL1A faster than any cytokine target since IL-twenty-three.

Fifth, the FDA reversed its February complete response on Regenxbio's NAVSUNLI — clemidsogene lanparvovec, R-G-X-one-twenty-one — the company's gene therapy for Hunter syndrome, MPS-two. The agency now says the existing clinical dataset is sufficient for accelerated approval; no new enrollment, no new studies. Regenxbio resubmits the BLA in the third quarter. Stock up thirty-seven percent premarket on the news. This is the second high-profile gene-therapy CRL reversal in six months and reinforces that the new FDA cell-and-gene-therapy posture — out of the Center for Biologics under the operational reforms announced last month — is materially more permissive than the prior regime. Read-through to any gene-therapy program in the Foresite portfolio carrying a near-term filing.

Sixth and on the regulatory backdrop. HHS rolled out Operation Trial Blazer at the BIO opening yesterday — an FDA-coordinated initiative to compress Phase 1 IND-clearance-to-first-patient timelines by six to twelve months, plus two proof-of-concept real-time clinical trials reporting endpoints live to the agency. It's the operationalization of the one-pivotal-trial-is-default Makary-Prasad NEJM editorial from earlier this year and pairs with the cell-and-gene draft guidance that dropped two weeks ago. Net effect: U.S. early-clinical timelines are now an explicit competition vehicle against China BD, and that's the framing every Calibr program in IND-enabling work should price into its twenty twenty-seven planning.
