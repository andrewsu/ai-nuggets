Good morning, Travis. Tuesday June thirtieth, twenty twenty-six. Today's roundup leads with Zymeworks's nine-hundred-twenty-nine-million-dollar take-out of Theravance Biopharma announced last night, walks through Abivax's obefazimod ulcerative colitis safety package that landed Monday evening and pushed the stock up roughly twenty-five percent into Tuesday — that's the spotlight in a few hours — covers BioCryst winding down internal discovery and closing its Birmingham Alabama research facility, Talawar Therapeutics's two-hundred-eighty-five-million-dollar JATT-Two SPAC merger for an I-L-thirteen by I-L-eighteen bispecific in atopic dermatitis, and the departure of Vijay Kumar as acting director of the F-D-A's Office of Therapeutic Products. Five items.

First, Zymeworks announced last night that it has agreed to acquire Theravance Biopharma for approximately nine-hundred-twenty-nine million dollars in equity value — seventeen dollars per share in cash, a twenty-two-percent premium, plus a contingent value right tied to ampreloxetine. Three threads. One, this is a post-clinical-setback target. Theravance's lead pipeline asset — ampreloxetine for neurogenic orthostatic hypotension — failed its Phase three CYPRESS trial earlier this year. The C-V-R returns eighty percent of any future ampreloxetine net proceeds to legacy shareholders if Zymeworks eventually licenses or sells it. Two, the asset Zymeworks is actually buying is Yupelri — the once-daily nebulized long-acting muscarinic antagonist for C-O-P-D maintenance partnered with Viatris — and the royalty stream from Trelegy co-developed with G-S-K. Yupelri did seventy-five million dollars in twenty twenty-five. Three, the deal structure is the read for Zymeworks. The company is financing the transaction with three-hundred-fifty million dollars of non-recourse debt from O-M-E-R-S Life Sciences secured by Yupelri profit share, three-hundred-sixty million dollars of Theravance's expected closing cash on hand, and just two-hundred-nineteen million dollars from Zymeworks's own balance sheet. Zymeworks — historically an oncology-focused antibody-engineering platform — is explicitly reframing itself as a royalty-management platform layering near-term cash-flowing assets on top of the core engineering franchise. The transaction is expected to be immediately accretive on close and Zymeworks is continuing its one-hundred-twenty-five million dollar buyback program. Closing is targeted for the second half this year. This is a different model for biotech M-and-A from what we've seen in the AbbVie-Apogee and Ipsen-Kartos comps — Zymeworks is not buying late-stage pipeline; it is buying durable royalty cash flow at a market-cap multiple, with the underlying clinical thesis already de-risked.

Second — and this is the spotlight in a few hours — Abivax reported topline results from ABTECT Maintenance Part Two and the expanded Phase three safety database for obefazimod in moderately-to-severely active ulcerative colitis Monday evening, and the stock is up roughly twenty-five percent overnight into Tuesday morning. The data drop matters because of what happened on June first. ABTECT Maintenance Part One produced placebo-adjusted clinical remission rates of forty percentage points — extraordinary numbers that analysts called best-in-disease — but alongside a handful of malignancy cases, mostly skin cancers, that crushed the stock more than forty percent on the day and triggered a Jefferies downgrade. Last night's Part Two expanded the safety database in the refractory population. The new malignancy cases — four non-melanoma skin cancers and two other malignancies, all in patients with established risk factors and adjudicated unrelated to treatment — pencilled out at exposure-adjusted incidence rates of zero-point-three-five to zero-point-six-nine per one-hundred patient-years for malignancies excluding non-melanoma skin cancer, comfortably within the published zero-point-three-zero to zero-point-seven-zero U-C background reference range. The efficacy data were, if anything, stronger than Part One. Thirty-seven-point-two percent of induction non-responders achieved clinical remission at Week forty-four on continued fifty milligram treatment, and dose escalation to fifty milligrams recaptured clinical remission in forty-five-point-five percent of patients who relapsed during Part One. N-D-A submission remains on track for Q-four this year. Third, BioCryst Pharmaceuticals announced Monday morning that it is winding down internal drug-discovery programs and closing its Birmingham Alabama research facility by year-end. The company is reframing as a commercial-stage rare-disease specialist pivoting fully to external innovation. Two consequences. One, twenty twenty-six adjusted operating expense guidance comes down to four-twenty to four-forty million dollars from prior four-fifty to four-seventy. The Orladeyo H-A-E revenue guide holds at six-twenty-five to six-forty-five million dollars and total revenue at six-thirty-five to six-sixty. Two, the late-stage pipeline narrows to two assets — navenibart for H-A-E with Phase three topline expected Q-three twenty twenty-seven, and B-C-X-one-seven-seven-two-five for Netherton syndrome with proof-of-concept data expected by year-end. The read across the sector is that mid-cap biotechs with one commercial asset and several Phase one programs are concluding that running internal discovery does not pencil out against the partnership-and-acquisition cost curve. For Calibr-Skaggs the read is that the in-licensing market for late-discovery and early-development assets is structurally tightening into a buyer's market as more mid-caps unload internal pipelines — partnership conversations on Calibr-discovered assets should price that in.

Fourth, Talawar Therapeutics — the first vehicle to commercialize assets discovered by Khanda Therapeutics — announced Monday a definitive business combination agreement with JATT-Two Acquisition that takes the company public on Nasdaq under the ticker T-L-W-R. The package includes an oversubscribed two-hundred-twenty-five million dollar PIPE led by Access Biotechnology with total expected proceeds of two-hundred-eighty-five million dollars at closing. The lead asset is TALA-one-twenty-five, an anti-I-L-thirteen by anti-I-L-eighteen bispecific antibody for atopic dermatitis. The clinical thesis is that pairing the I-L-thirteen pathway — clinically validated by Dupixent — with the I-L-eighteen pathway — being clinically derisked through Apollo Therapeutics's camoteskimab Phase two-a CHAMELEON trial — into a single bispecific molecule clears the monotherapy efficacy ceiling that anti-I-L-thirteen agents are running into. Clinic entry is targeted for Q-one twenty twenty-seven, interim Phase one data Q-four twenty twenty-seven, and the cash position funds the asset through a Phase two-b proof-of-concept readout in the second half of twenty twenty-eight. Talawar is the third bispecific-platform I-and-I SPAC merger this calendar year — the validation pattern for two-orthogonal-pathway immunology bispecifics is now well-established.

Fifth and shortest, Vijay Kumar — acting director of the F-D-A's Office of Therapeutic Products, the C-B-E-R office responsible for cell and gene therapy reviews — confirmed Monday in an internal email that he will step down. Kumar had been acting director for roughly a year, since C-B-E-R Director Vinay Prasad removed his predecessor Nicole Verdun. No successor has been named. The departure continues the leadership turbulence at C-B-E-R under the current Prasad-led era and adds another decision-point of regulatory uncertainty for cell and gene therapy sponsors. For Foresite-portfolio cell and gene therapy programs targeting accelerated approval in the next twelve months, watch the interim leadership succession and any signal it sends on review-team continuity for in-flight applications.
