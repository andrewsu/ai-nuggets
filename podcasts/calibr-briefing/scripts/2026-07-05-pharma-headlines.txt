First, Novo Nordisk Wegovy UK MASH. Friday the UK MHRA granted conditional marketing authorization to Novo Nordisk's Wegovy — semaglutide — for adults with non-cirrhotic metabolic-associated steatohepatitis and moderate-to-advanced liver fibrosis. Standard maintenance dose two-point-four milligrams weekly, with escalation to seven-point-two milligrams weekly permitted in obese patients. Conditional means the National Institute for Health and Care Excellence has to complete its cost-effectiveness appraisal before the NHS will reimburse. Two reads. One, this is the first GLP-one to carry a MASH label outside the U-S, coming almost a year after F-D-A accelerated-approved semaglutide for the same indication in August twenty twenty-five on the ESSENCE Phase three trial — sixty-two-point-nine percent versus thirty-four-point-three percent steatohepatitis resolution at seventy-two weeks, twenty-eight-point-seven percent estimated difference in responder proportions with p less than zero-point-zero-zero-one. The U-K conditional approval extends Wegovy's franchise into a third indication — obesity, cardiovascular risk reduction, and now MASH — and validates the metabolic-mechanism-upstream approach to fibrotic liver disease. Two, for the MASH-therapeutic class competitively, Madrigal's Rezdiffra — the T-H-R-beta agonist that carries the first-in-class F-D-A MASH label since March twenty twenty-four — remains the reference-case MASH-native compound. Wegovy competes not on hepatocyte-directed signaling but on metabolic-and-weight-loss upstream, which is a different value proposition and probably a different patient — obese patients with MASH getting GLP-one, non-obese or GLP-one-intolerant patients getting Rezdiffra. Deep dive in a few hours.

Second, Brii Biosciences hepatitis B. Thursday Brii Biosciences reported end-of-treatment data from two Phase two-b studies in chronic hepatitis B. The ENRICH study — BRII-one-seventy-nine, a recombinant protein-based H-B-V immunotherapeutic expressing Pre-S-one, Pre-S-two, and S surface antigens, administered as a priming immunotherapy before elebsiran, the Vir-licensed siRNA that degrades H-B-V R-N-A, plus PEG-interferon-alpha — delivered surface-antigen loss rates of forty-two-point-nine percent on a five-dose schedule in ninety-eight patients and forty percent on a seven-dose schedule in fifty patients, against ten-point-two percent for PEG-interferon-alpha alone. The ENHANCE study — the concurrent triple — hit twenty-five-point-five percent in the full-course arm and twenty-two-point-five percent in the shortened-PEG arm, both underperforming ENSURE-Cohort-two-and-three benchmarks around twenty-nine-point-seven percent. Brii selected the ENRICH sequential design for Phase three. Two reads. One, the sequential-priming-versus-concurrent-triple contrast is a clean read on how to sequence a functional-cure regimen: BRII-one-seventy-nine has to run first — the immune priming needs to build B-cell and T-cell responses before the siRNA plus interferon reduces the surface-antigen antigen load, not alongside it. Forty-plus-percent H-B-sAg loss versus ten percent for PEG-interferon monotherapy is the largest immunotherapy-differentiated signal in H-B-V functional-cure development so far. Two, the arbitration overhang. Brii explicitly disclosed that advancing ENRICH to Phase three is contingent on the outcome of arbitration with Vir Biotechnology over the elebsiran manufacturing technology transfer. That is a live commercial-tension situation between the licensor and licensee on a shared functional-cure asset, and it caps Brii's near-term de-risking event on a legal outcome rather than a clinical one. For the H-B-V-functional-cure class broadly, this is the first time a two-arm same-triple sequential-versus-concurrent comparison has produced a decisive readout — the emerging playbook is priming-then-antiviral rather than everything-at-once.

Third, Fosun-Junshi roconkibart. Thursday a Fosun Pharma subsidiary paid Junshi Biosciences thirty-two million dollars upfront — two-hundred-fifteen million yuan — for exclusive greater-China rights to roconkibart, also known as J-S-oh-oh-five, an anti-interleukin-seventeen-A monoclonal antibody at N-M-P-A filing for moderate-to-severe plaque psoriasis. Deal is structured with up to twenty-seven million dollars in development milestones, up to one-hundred-thirty-nine million in sales milestones, and tiered double-digit royalties. Phase three delivered a ninety-one percent P-A-S-I-ninety response at sixteen weeks and sixty-five percent P-A-S-I-one-hundred at fifty-two weeks. Two reads. One, roconkibart's efficacy numbers are competitive with second-generation dual-I-L-seventeen agents like U-C-B's Bimzelx — the P-A-S-I-one-hundred at fifty-two weeks is the meaningful benchmark for durability in psoriasis, and roconkibart at sixty-five percent reads as class-leading against selective anti-I-L-seventeen-A monoclonals like Cosentyx and Taltz where P-A-S-I-one-hundred typically runs forty to fifty percent. That said, the deal price is small — thirty-two million upfront for a China-only license on a Phase-three-complete asset with N-M-P-A imminent tells you domestic Chinese I-L-seventeen pricing is not aggressive. Two, for the licensor Junshi Biosciences, this is a non-oncology asset converted into cash and Fosun sales-and-marketing execution — Junshi is preserving R-and-D capacity for its core P-D-one franchise (J-S-zero-zero-one, toripalimab) against competitive pressure from Innovent, BeiGene, and biosimilars. For Foresite, the macro read is Chinese biotechs increasingly willing to license out non-core assets to domestic partners at modest deal sizes as a way to fund core-pipeline execution — a template that shows up in Fosun's expanding deal book.

Fourth, Celea Therapeutics day two. Following Thursday's headline coverage of the one-hundred-eighty-million-dollar Celea financing to launch SURPASS-I-P-F, the additional context worth flagging is on the deuteration mechanism and the Phase two-b substrate. Deupirfenidone is pirfenidone with hydrogen-to-deuterium substitution at metabolic-hotspot positions, creating a kinetic isotope effect that slows cytochrome-P-four-fifty oxidation and attenuates the peak-to-trough plasma concentration swing. Phase two-b ELEVATE-I-P-F was a two-hundred-fifty-seven-patient four-arm study — deupirfenidone at two doses, pirfenidone eight-hundred-one milligrams three-times-daily, or placebo — dosed twenty-six weeks. Deupirfenidone eight-hundred-twenty-five milligrams three-times-daily delivered roughly fifty-percent-higher drug exposure than pirfenidone with adverse-event incidence at eighty-five-point-nine percent versus pirfenidone's eighty-four-point-one percent — statistically indistinguishable. Forced vital capacity decline over twenty-six weeks on the eight-twenty-five arm was negative twenty-one-and-a-half milliliters versus negative one-hundred-twelve-point-five on placebo. Two reads. One, negative twenty-one-and-a-half milliliters over twenty-six weeks is roughly the normal-aging F-V-C-decline rate — the mechanistic goal of every antifibrotic in the class has been to bend the curve toward normal aging, and the Phase two-b point estimate does that. Two, SURPASS-I-P-F is powered for superiority against pirfenidone at week fifty-two on absolute F-V-C change — a bet-the-company Phase three design that skips the pirfenidone-plus-add-on framing and goes straight for standard-of-care displacement. If it hits, the twenty-five-percent U-S I-P-F antifibrotic penetration rate has room to at least double, because the tolerability-ceiling constraint disappears.

Fifth, United Therapeutics-Thymmune day two. Following Thursday's headline coverage of United Therapeutics' one-hundred-forty-million-dollar acquisition of Thymmune, the additional context worth flagging is the strategic fit with the UThymoKidney xenotransplant program. Thymmune's iPSC-derived thymic cell therapy converts human induced-pluripotent stem cells into thymic epithelial cells that mature in-vivo into a functional neo-thymus supporting T-cell development. UThymoKidney is Rothblatt's genetically-modified porcine kidney plus autologous thymic co-graft protocol for xenotransplantation-with-tolerance-induction — the thymus co-graft is what enables the recipient to develop a self-tolerant T-cell repertoire that doesn't reject the xenograft. Two reads. One, iPSC-derived thymic cells become a synthetic-biology replacement for the porcine thymic co-graft — same tolerance-induction premise, but the T-cell education substrate is a scalable human iPSC-derived product rather than a xenogenic tissue graft. The two together are a two-pillar tolerance-induction strategy: the xeno-kidney provides the organ, the iPSC-thymic-cell provides tolerance. Two, for the modality as a category — iPSC-derived thymic cell therapy is essentially a one-precedent business against Enzyvant's Rethymic donor-derived thymus tissue implant, which got F-D-A approval in twenty twenty-one for congenital athymia. Thymmune's iPSC route replaces donor tissue with an off-the-shelf scalable stem-cell product; if it clears the same regulatory bar, it becomes an on-demand supply that opens transplant-tolerance and immune-restoration indications well beyond congenital athymia. The one-hundred-forty-million-dollar entry price is small relative to what a scalable congenital-athymia franchise plus downstream transplant-tolerance play could be worth.
