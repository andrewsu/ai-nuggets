First, the Biomni general-purpose biomedical A-I agent. On Thursday July ninth, Kexin Huang and Jure Leskovec's group at Stanford published in Science a paper titled Autonomous Biomedical Research with an Artificial Intelligence Agent. Biomni is an open-source generalist agentic system that integrates large-language-model reasoning, retrieval-augmented planning, and code-based execution — pulling on a hundred-fifty-plus bioinformatics tools, fifty-nine curated databases, and a hundred-plus molecular-modeling software packages, all discovered automatically by an action-discovery agent that mined tools and protocols from tens of thousands of publications across twenty-five biomedical subfields. On a systematic four-hundred-forty-three-question benchmark it outperforms general large language models with zero task-specific prompt tuning, and demonstrates end-to-end workflows in causal gene prioritization, drug repurposing, rare-disease diagnosis, microbiome analysis, and molecular cloning. The prototype has already been used by fifteen thousand scientists in a hundred-thousand different workflows over nine months. In September twenty twenty-five the team spun the platform out into Phylo, a Palo-Alto startup led by C-E-O Kexin Huang and President Yuanhao Qu with scientific cofounders Jure Leskovec at Stanford C-S and Le Cong at Stanford Pathology, and a scientific advisory board that includes Nobel laureate Carolyn Bertozzi and CRISPR pioneer Feng Zhang. In February twenty twenty-six Phylo closed a thirteen-and-a-half-million-dollar seed round co-led by Andreessen Horowitz and Menlo Ventures — Menlo through the Anthropic-linked Anthology fund — with Zetta, Conviction, S-V Angel, and Valkyrie joining. Second, the Leqembi subcutaneous autoinjector A-A-I-C data. Sunday July twelfth Eisai and Biogen — with Swedish co-inventor BioArctic — presented data at A-A-I-C twenty twenty-six in London showing that the once-weekly five-hundred-milligram Leqembi subcutaneous autoinjector delivers drug exposure bioequivalent to the approved intravenous ten-milligram-per-kilogram-every-two-weeks initiation regimen at an exposure ratio of one-hundred-four percent, with amyloid removal, clinical efficacy, and A-R-I-A-E incidence all driven by exposure rather than route of administration. Anti-drug-antibody incidence was one-point-four percent and no neutralizing antibodies were observed. Patient and care-partner satisfaction ranged seventy-five to ninety-seven percent, and willingness to recommend ranged ninety-two to one-hundred percent. The subcutaneous maintenance formulation was already F-D-A-approved in August twenty twenty-five; the subcutaneous initiation supplemental B-L-A is under F-D-A Priority Review with an extended P-D-U-F-A of August twenty-fourth, and Japan filed in November twenty twenty-five and China granted Priority Review in January twenty twenty-six. Two reads. One, this is the anti-amyloid equivalent of the Sarclisa Escena on-body-injector oncology-biologics story Sanofi got approved Thursday — same commercial life-cycle-management play, different franchise, and a defensive move against Eli Lilly's Kisunla donanemab in the amyloid market. Two, autoinjector delivery is now the standard playbook for anti-amyloid class competitiveness and could re-open primary-care Alzheimer's prescribing that has been stalled by infusion-chair capacity constraints.

Third, Roche's Huntington's-disease double drop. Thursday July ninth Roche informed the Huntington's community that it is ending development of both tominersen — the huntingtin-lowering antisense oligonucleotide licensed from Ionis in twenty seventeen — and of R-G-six-four-nine-six, its early-stage sibling molecule. In the pivotal Phase 2 GENERATION-HD-two trial the tominersen arm met its safety endpoint and its huntingtin-protein-lowering biomarker endpoint — meaning the drug did what it said it does mechanistically — but at sixteen months the mutant-protein reduction did not translate into a clinically meaningful improvement in disease severity, and Roche pulled the plug. The Ionis read is brutal. This is Ionis's second major failure in one week after AstraZeneca-Ionis's Wainua eplontersen missed the primary endpoint in the CARDIO-T-T-Ransform Phase 3 in T-T-R amyloid cardiomyopathy Wednesday July eighth. Ionis shares are down about twenty-nine percent over five sessions, and the A-S-O platform is now under harder scrutiny — the Kynamro-Kynamro-Miloxin-follow-up-Wainua-tominersen chain is a rough sequence for a class-defining company. For the broader Huntington's-disease field this closes the biggest single-target-lowering shot on goal for a generation; U-niQure, PTC Therapeutics, Wave Life Sciences, and A-D-N-A-Prime programs are now the front-runners, and the H-D community has to wait longer.

Fourth, the GSK-Alector progranulin-antibody partnership termination. By written notice dated Monday July sixth GSK ended its collaboration and license agreement with Alector covering the two progranulin-elevating anti-sortilin monoclonal antibodies, latozinemab and nivisnebart. The termination is effective one-hundred-eighty days after the notice, so January second twenty twenty-seven. The July twenty twenty-one agreement had a seven-hundred-million-dollar upfront, of which GSK will now recover the sixty-percent share of nivisnebart development costs GSK has been carrying since the Phase 2 PROGRESS-A-D interim futility hit in April twenty twenty-six. The precipitating events. Phase 3 INFRONT-three of latozinemab in frontotemporal dementia due to a G-R-N gene mutation missed its primary endpoint in October twenty twenty-five, and Alector's twenty twenty-five decision to stop the open-label extension already telegraphed the failure. Phase 2 PROGRESS-A-D of nivisnebart in early Alzheimer's hit interim futility in April twenty twenty-six on primary-endpoint disease-progression-slowing likelihood. That is the second consecutive miss on the progranulin-elevation hypothesis in neurodegeneration, and GSK — post-Alector — is now down to Blenrep and its ADC portfolio as the neuroscience-adjacent oncology narrative and does not have a lead neurodegeneration late-stage asset. Alector cut its workforce forty-nine percent in the aftermath and is refocusing on non-progranulin programs including its transferrin-receptor brain-shuttle A-B-C-platform. The read for the progranulin-elevation-in-neurodegeneration hypothesis is that it is now essentially dead as a monotherapy story — two large Phase 3-slash-Phase 2 misses in a generation-limited genetic-target space is a hard signal.

And fifth, INmune Bio's X-Pro Phase 2 imaging data at A-A-I-C. Sunday July twelfth INmune Bio presented at A-A-I-C twenty twenty-six positive Phase 2 imaging data for X-Pro, its investigational soluble-tumor-necrosis-factor-alpha inhibitor, in early Alzheimer's disease. The result. In a randomized placebo-controlled Phase 2 trial of one-hundred-plus patients — the MINDful-A-D study — X-Pro delivered a statistically significant treatment effect at p less-than-zero-point-oh-one on a white-matter-myelin biomarker measured by chi-separation M-R-I in the modified intent-to-treat population at week twenty-four. Cortical-disarray-measurement gray-matter imaging showed a concordant effect. The mechanism story. X-Pro is a soluble-T-N-F selective inhibitor that does not touch transmembrane T-N-F — leaving intact the immune-protective transmembrane signaling that would otherwise be a class-limiting toxicity for pan-T-N-F inhibitors like the R-A-approved anti-T-N-F antibodies. The bet is that neuroinflammation is a driver rather than a bystander in early A-D, and that shutting off soluble T-N-F preserves white matter and cortical microstructure. The bigger picture. This is the second week in a row A-A-I-C has produced a positive Phase 2 neuroinflammation-in-A-D imaging readout — Cassava Sciences' simufilam post-hoc analysis last year has been the anti-example — and if the Phase 3 M-I-N-D-A-D-two trial reads out with cognitive-benefit signal, X-Pro would validate neuroinflammation as an addressable target class beyond anti-amyloid.

Vaccinex is presenting new S-E-M-A-four-D-antibody pepinemab biomarker data at A-A-I-C later this morning laying out the Phase 2-b SIGNAL-A-D-two design in early Alzheimer's — that is the second neuroinflammation-mechanism A-A-I-C readout to watch this afternoon.
